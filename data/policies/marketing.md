## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes how Meridian Trust Bank, N.A. ("the Bank") plans, reviews, approves, distributes, monitors, and retains marketing and advertising for the Bank's consumer and business products and services. The policy is designed to comply with the Bank's overarching prohibition on unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices (Dodd-Frank Sections 1031 and 1036, 12 U.S.C. 5531 and 5536, as elaborated in the CFPB Supervision and Examination Manual), Regulation DD (12 C.F.R. 1030.8), Regulation Z (12 C.F.R. 1026.16 open-end and 1026.24 closed-end), Regulation E (12 C.F.R. 1005.7 and 1005.18 prepaid), Regulation B (12 C.F.R. 1002.4), the FCRA prescreened-solicitation framework (15 U.S.C. 1681m / 12 C.F.R. Part 1022 Subpart D), the TCPA and FCC Do-Not-Call regulations (47 U.S.C. 227 / 47 C.F.R. 64.1200), the CAN-SPAM Act and FTC implementing regulations (15 U.S.C. 7701 / 16 C.F.R. Part 316), the FFIEC Consumer Compliance Risk Management Guidance on Social Media (December 11, 2013), the FTC Endorsement Guides (16 C.F.R. Part 255), and applicable fair lending considerations under POL-FAIR-001.

Marketing reviews, exception logs, advertising-related complaint trends, and Do-Not-Call / CAN-SPAM suppression metrics are reported to the Office of Consumer Compliance, the Consumer Compliance Committee, and (on an aggregate basis) the Board of Directors through the consumer compliance program.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to every marketing or advertising communication, in any medium, that promotes a Bank product, service, brand, or institutional relationship to a consumer, prospective consumer, business customer, or prospective business customer. Covered media include print (statement inserts, brochures, direct mail, newspaper, magazine, billboards), broadcast (radio, television, streaming audio and video, podcast read-ads), digital (Bank website, mobile app, display / search / programmatic ads, paid and organic social media, influencer / endorser content, sponsored email newsletters, push notifications), telephone (outbound dialing, recorded messages, SMS / text messaging), email (commercial email subject to CAN-SPAM and transactional email bundled with promotional content), and in-branch media (lobby signage, teller-line displays, ATM screens, on-hold messaging).

It applies to every Bank employee, officer, contractor, agency, vendor, affiliate, and authorized third party (marketing agencies, lead generators, list brokers, telemarketing vendors, email service providers, social media management vendors, and compensated influencers) that creates, reviews, approves, distributes, or monitors a covered communication. Vendor relationships are governed under POL-VRM-001 with this policy's substantive content rules flowed down by written agreement.

This policy supersedes any prior marketing or advertising policy. Where this policy conflicts with applicable law, regulation, or supervisory guidance, the law or guidance controls. Domain-specific policies (POL-FAIR-001 fair lending; POL-COMP-001 consumer complaints; POL-REGE-001 Reg E; POL-OD-001 overdraft; POL-VRM-001 vendor management; POL-IS-001 information security; POL-UDAAP-001 UDAAP) operate within this framework and inherit its definitions where consistent.

## 3. Definitions

**Advertisement** means a commercial message, in any medium, that promotes a Bank product or service or the Bank's brand or institutional relationships in a way that names or implies any product or service. The term has the operative meaning of 12 C.F.R. 1030.2(b) for deposit accounts and 12 C.F.R. 1026.2(a)(2) for consumer credit, and is read consistent with their staff commentary.

**Triggering Term** means a term whose use in an Advertisement triggers additional disclosure under Reg DD (e.g., "bonus", "APY"), Reg Z open-end (a finance charge, periodic rate, or other charge under 1026.16(b)), or Reg Z closed-end (a payment amount, finance charge, or down payment under 1026.24(d)). When a Triggering Term appears, the additional disclosures required by the implicated rule must appear with at least equal prominence and in close proximity in the form and content prescribed by that rule.

**UDAAP** means an unfair, deceptive, or abusive act or practice under Sections 1031 and 1036 of the Dodd-Frank Act (12 U.S.C. 5531, 5536) and the CFPB Supervision and Examination Manual. An act or practice is **unfair** if it causes or is likely to cause substantial consumer injury that is not reasonably avoidable and is not outweighed by countervailing benefits; **deceptive** if it contains a material representation, omission, or practice that misleads or is likely to mislead a reasonable consumer acting reasonably; and **abusive** if it materially interferes with a consumer's ability to understand a term or condition or takes unreasonable advantage of a consumer's lack of understanding, inability to protect their own interests, or reasonable reliance on the Bank.

**Prescreened Solicitation** means an offer of credit or insurance not initiated by the consumer, made based on information from a consumer report obtained under 15 U.S.C. 1681b(c), and subject to 15 U.S.C. 1681m(d) and 12 C.F.R. Part 1022 Subpart D (firm-offer requirement, model opt-out notice, and the nationwide-consumer-reporting-agency toll-free / website opt-out infrastructure).

**Telephone Solicitation** has the meaning given in 47 C.F.R. 64.1200(f)(15): a telephone call or message initiated to encourage the purchase or rental of, or investment in, property, goods, or services, with the rule's enumerated exceptions (prior express invitation or permission, established business relationship, tax-exempt nonprofit).

**Established Business Relationship (EBR)** has the meaning of 47 C.F.R. 64.1200(f)(5): a prior or existing voluntary two-way relationship based on an inquiry, application, purchase, or transaction not terminated by either party, sunsetting 18 months after the last transaction or 3 months after the last inquiry.

**Commercial Electronic Mail Message** has the meaning of 15 U.S.C. 7702(2) and 16 C.F.R. 316.3: an electronic mail message whose primary purpose is the commercial advertisement or promotion of a commercial product or service. Primary-purpose classification of mixed transactional / commercial messages follows 16 C.F.R. 316.3.

**Endorsement** has the meaning of 16 C.F.R. 255.0(b): an advertising message that consumers are likely to believe reflects the opinions, beliefs, findings, or experiences of a party other than the sponsoring advertiser. **Material Connection** has the meaning of 16 C.F.R. 255.5: any connection between an Endorser and the advertiser that might materially affect the weight or credibility of the Endorsement and that would not reasonably be expected by the audience (payment, free product, employment relationship, family relationship, or similar).

## 4. Governance and Roles

The **Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)** owns the marketing program operationally and designates a **Marketing Compliance Liaison** as the day-to-day point of contact for compliance review. The **Office of Consumer Compliance**, led by the Chief Compliance Officer, owns substantive compliance review for UDAAP, Reg DD, Reg Z, Reg E, Reg B, FCRA prescreening, TCPA, CAN-SPAM, social media, and endorsement rules, and has unilateral authority to require changes or withhold approval. Disputes between Marketing and Compliance escalate to the General Counsel, whose decision controls.

The **Office of the General Counsel (Legal)** reviews elevated-risk materials: prescreened solicitations; sweepstakes, contests, and promotions involving consideration; communications referencing third-party trademarks; communications using endorsements with Material Connections; communications targeted to military servicemembers; and any communication flagged as elevated risk by Compliance. The **Fair Lending Officer** (under POL-FAIR-001) reviews credit-product advertising for redlining risk, equal-credit-opportunity messaging, and media-market and audience-targeting decisions. The **BSA Officer** (POL-BSA-001) reviews Account-opening incentive offers for structuring or layering risk. The **Information Security Officer** (POL-IS-001) reviews any communication that requests authentication credentials, account numbers, or other Sensitive Customer Information.

Marketing communications, exception logs, and metric trends are reported quarterly to the Consumer Compliance Committee; material adverse findings (deceptive-claim takedown, TCPA violation, FTC or state-AG inquiry) are reported promptly to the General Counsel and the Board's Risk Committee.

## 5. Pre-Publication Review Workflow

No covered communication may be distributed, published, posted, mailed, broadcast, or otherwise disseminated until it has completed the pre-publication review workflow. The workflow is captured in the Bank's marketing review platform and produces an auditable record sufficient to reconstruct who reviewed what, when, with what comments, and with what disposition.

The workflow has the following sequential stages: (a) **Brief** -- Marketing documents the campaign objective, target audience, channels, claims, calls to action, offer terms, and product references; (b) **Draft Production** -- Marketing or its agency produces draft content; (c) **Marketing Self-Check** -- the Marketing Compliance Liaison runs the Bank's product/channel checklist for required disclosures, prominence, and contact information; (d) **Compliance Review** -- the Office of Consumer Compliance reviews for UDAAP, Reg DD, Reg Z, Reg E, Reg B, FCRA, TCPA, CAN-SPAM, social media, endorsement, and fair lending issues; (e) **Specialty Reviews** -- Legal, Fair Lending, BSA, and/or Information Security review as required by Section 4; (f) **Final Sign-Off** -- Compliance issues an Approved / Approved with Required Changes / Disapproved disposition keyed to a specific final asset version; and (g) **Distribution** -- the asset is released to the channel only against the approved version.

A communication may not be distributed under a verbal approval, soft approval, or approval keyed to a different asset version. Material changes to an approved asset (substantive copy, offer terms, disclosure language, channel scope, or campaign-window extension beyond the approved end date) require re-submission and re-approval before further distribution. Non-material edits (typo correction, color adjustments that do not affect a Triggering Term or required disclosure) may be made under a documented Marketing Compliance Liaison sign-off.

## 6. UDAAP Standards for Substantive Claims

Every covered communication must satisfy the UDAAP standard. Compliance applies the unfair / deceptive / abusive framework defined in Section 3 to the net impression created by the communication, judged by the impression of a reasonable consumer in the target audience and taking into account medium, audience, and qualifications -- not by literal parsing of words.

Specific prohibitions: no claim of "free", "no fee", or "no cost" if any condition (minimum balance, qualifying activity, time period) must be met to avoid a fee, unless the condition is disclosed with at least equal prominence and in close proximity; no claim of "guaranteed approval" for a credit product subject to underwriting; no representation that opt-in to a service (including the Reg E 1005.17 overdraft opt-in under POL-OD-001) is required, recommended, or default; no peer / "lowest" / "best" / "highest" comparison or superlative without a documented, current, comparable substantiation source; no fine-print contradiction of a headline claim; no testimonial or endorsement that misrepresents typical results; and no targeting of communications to populations selected on vulnerability characteristics not relevant to the offered product (e.g., targeting high-cost overdraft promotions to recipients of public-assistance deposits, addressed in POL-OD-001).

Substantiation files are required for any factual, comparative, ranking, or rate claim, must be on file at approval, and are retained per Section 15.

## 7. Reg DD Deposit Advertising (12 C.F.R. 1030.8)

Deposit-account Advertisements must comply with Regulation DD's advertising rules (12 C.F.R. 1030.8) and may not be misleading or inaccurate. The term "free" or "no cost" (or words of similar meaning) may not be used if any maintenance or activity fee may be imposed; non-maintenance / non-activity fees (such as a stop-payment fee) do not by themselves preclude use of "free" but must not be obscured. The annual percentage yield (APY) must be stated using the term "annual percentage yield" (which may be abbreviated as "APY"); the interest rate, if used, must be stated as the "annual percentage rate" or "interest rate" with prominence not greater than the APY.

If the Advertisement states the APY, it must also disclose the period during which the APY is offered (or that the rate is accurate as of a specific date), any minimum balance required to obtain the APY, any minimum opening deposit if greater than the minimum balance required to obtain the APY, the effect of fees on the APY where fees may reduce earnings, and any required features of a time account (term, early-withdrawal-penalty existence). Use of a Triggering Term such as "bonus" requires disclosure of the "annual percentage yield", any minimum balance / time / activity required to obtain the bonus, the time at which the bonus will be provided, and the form of the bonus.

Indoor sign exemptions under 12 C.F.R. 1030.8(e)(1) are used sparingly and only with Compliance pre-approval; the broadcast / electronic media short-form exemption under 12 C.F.R. 1030.8(e)(2) is used only when the audio script directs the consumer to a number or location at which full disclosures are available.

## 8. Reg Z Credit Advertising (12 C.F.R. 1026.16 and 1026.24)

Open-end credit Advertisements (e.g., credit card, home equity line of credit) comply with 12 C.F.R. 1026.16. Use of any 1026.16(b) Triggering Term (a finance charge, periodic rate, or other charge) triggers the additional disclosures required by 1026.16(b)(1). The annual percentage rate must use the term "annual percentage rate" (which may be abbreviated as "APR"); a periodic rate may not be used in lieu of an APR. Home-equity-line advertising further complies with 1026.16(d) (additional disclosures for HELOCs, including any rate-fluctuation, balloon, or tax-deductibility statement).

Closed-end credit Advertisements (e.g., auto loan, residential mortgage loan, personal installment loan) comply with 12 C.F.R. 1026.24. Stating any 1026.24(d)(1) Triggering Term (the amount or percentage of any down payment, the number of payments or period of repayment, the amount of any payment, or the amount of any finance charge) triggers the 1026.24(d)(2) additional disclosures (the amount or percentage of the down payment, the terms of repayment that reflect the repayment obligation over the full term of the loan, and the APR using that term, with a statement that the rate may be increased after consummation if applicable). Mortgage-loan advertising further complies with 1026.24(f) (no misleading rate or payment claim) and 1026.24(i) (specific prohibited acts and practices, including misrepresentations about government endorsement, debt-elimination claims, and similar claims).

## 9. Reg E and Prepaid Advertising (12 C.F.R. 1005.7 and 1005.18)

Advertisements that promote an electronic fund transfer service or a prepaid account include or direct the consumer to the disclosures required by 12 C.F.R. 1005.7 (initial disclosures) or 12 C.F.R. 1005.18(b) (prepaid pre-acquisition short and long forms). Prepaid pre-acquisition short-form and long-form disclosures are produced in the form and substance prescribed by 1005.18(b), including model forms (Appendix A-10(a) through A-10(e)), and may not be modified except as expressly permitted by the regulation.

## 10. Reg B Anti-Discriminatory Advertising (12 C.F.R. 1002.4)

Credit Advertisements may not, expressly or by implication, discourage on a prohibited basis a reasonable person from making or pursuing an application (12 C.F.R. 1002.4(b)). Imagery, language, target-list selection, and channel-mix decisions are reviewed by the Fair Lending Officer (under POL-FAIR-001) for redlining risk and for prohibited-basis discouragement. Equal Credit Opportunity Act notices required by 12 C.F.R. 1002.5(d) are not advertising disclosures, but the Bank's broader anti-discouragement posture under 1002.4 governs marketing-channel choices, including the selection of zip-code or census-tract targeting, the inclusion of all of the Bank's assessment area in market-saturation campaigns, and the avoidance of media outlets whose audience composition would, on the facts, exclude or discourage protected-class applicants.

## 11. FCRA Prescreened Solicitations (15 U.S.C. 1681m / 12 C.F.R. Part 1022 Subpart D)

A Prescreened Solicitation must constitute a firm offer of credit or insurance under 15 U.S.C. 1681a(l), with the offer's pre-selection criteria documented at the time of pre-screening and the offer honored if the consumer responds and continues to meet the pre-selection criteria and any post-application underwriting criteria established before the offer. Each Prescreened Solicitation must include the short notice and long notice required by 12 C.F.R. 1022.54, the notices must satisfy the form, content, and prominence requirements of that section (including the "PRESCREEN & OPT-OUT NOTICE" header in the short notice), and the toll-free number and website must be those operated by the nationwide consumer reporting agencies. The Bank suppresses recipients who appear on the consumer-reporting-agency-maintained opt-out list at the time of list selection. Solicitation documentation, criteria documentation, and the pre-selection / post-application criteria are retained as required by Section 14.

## 12. TCPA and Do-Not-Call (47 U.S.C. 227 / 47 C.F.R. 64.1200)

Telephone Solicitations and SMS / text marketing comply with the TCPA and 47 C.F.R. 64.1200. Auto-dialed or pre-recorded calls / texts to a wireless number require prior express written consent, except for calls within an EBR (subject to the FCC's express-written-consent carve-outs), manually placed calls outside the automatic-telephone-dialing-system definition, and emergency-purpose calls. Live-agent telemarketing to a residential line requires prior express invitation / permission or an EBR.

The Bank scrubs the National Do-Not-Call Registry no less frequently than every 31 days for any non-EBR campaign, maintains an internal Do-Not-Call list, honors requests within a reasonable time not to exceed 30 days, and retains the request for 5 years. Calling-time restrictions (no Telephone Solicitations before 8:00 a.m. or after 9:00 p.m. local time at the called party's location) are observed; caller-ID transmission is not blocked; the EBR sunsetting rule (Section 3) is observed. Telemarketing vendors are governed under POL-VRM-001 with the substantive rules of this policy, the scrub cadence, calling-time restrictions, and consent / EBR documentation flowed down by written agreement; vendor consent records are accessible within 1 business day.

## 13. CAN-SPAM Commercial Email (15 U.S.C. 7701 / 16 C.F.R. Part 316)

Commercial Electronic Mail Messages comply with the CAN-SPAM Act and 16 C.F.R. Part 316. Each message must (i) not contain false or misleading transmission information (header, "from", "reply-to", originating domain); (ii) not contain a deceptive subject line; (iii) clearly and conspicuously identify the message as an advertisement; (iv) include the Bank's valid physical postal address; (v) include a clear and conspicuous opt-out notice and a functional return email address or other internet-based opt-out mechanism; and (vi) honor opt-out requests for at least 30 days after the message is sent and within 10 business days of the request. The Bank does not transfer, sell, or rent its opt-out list; forward-to-a-friend functionality carries the same opt-out infrastructure and physical address. Transactional / relationship messages (16 C.F.R. 316.3(a)(2)) are not subject to the opt-out requirement; mixed-content messages whose primary purpose is commercial are. Primary-purpose classification follows 16 C.F.R. 316.3 and is documented in the marketing review record.

## 14. Social Media (FFIEC Consumer Compliance Risk Management Guidance, December 11, 2013) and Endorsements (16 C.F.R. Part 255)

Social media communications by, on behalf of, or attributable to the Bank are subject to this policy. The FFIEC Guidance's risk-management framework applies: a written social media risk-management program with clear roles, training, monitoring of Bank-attributable channels, response procedures for complaints raised through social media (escalated under POL-COMP-001), and oversight of third parties acting on the Bank's behalf. Substantive consumer-protection rules (UDAAP, Reg DD, Reg Z, Reg E, Reg B, FCRA, TCPA, CAN-SPAM, fair lending) apply to social media communications to the same extent as other media; the rules do not relax because the medium is short-form.

Endorsements and testimonials in any medium comply with 16 C.F.R. Part 255: they must reflect the honest opinions and experiences of the Endorser, may not convey representations that would be deceptive if made directly by the Bank, and must disclose any Material Connection clearly and conspicuously at or before the endorsement message in a placement and format the audience will notice and understand. Influencer agreements include explicit disclosure requirements, pre-clearance for material claims, and the Bank's right to require correction or deletion of non-compliant content. Compensated influencer content is treated as Bank-attributable for this policy and POL-FAIR-001. User-generated content republished or amplified by the Bank (re-shares, re-tweets, like-banner placements) is treated as Bank-attributable and reviewed before amplification.

## 15. Recordkeeping

Marketing records are retained as follows: Reg DD deposit Advertisements and supporting substantiation for at least 2 years (12 C.F.R. 1030.9(c)); Reg Z credit Advertisements and supporting substantiation for at least 2 years (12 C.F.R. 1026.25); Reg B credit-related marketing-channel and target-list documentation for at least 25 months for consumer credit and 12 months for business credit (consistent with 12 C.F.R. 1002.12 and POL-FAIR-001); FCRA Prescreened Solicitations and pre-selection / post-application criteria for the period required by 15 U.S.C. 1681m; TCPA consent records and Do-Not-Call requests for at least 5 years (47 C.F.R. 64.1200(d)(6)); CAN-SPAM opt-out records for the opt-out-honoring period and for 5 years thereafter; FFIEC social media monitoring records for at least 3 years; and marketing review records (briefs, reviews, sign-offs, exception logs, substantiation) for at least 3 years. Where retention periods conflict, the longer period controls.

## 16. Compliance Monitoring

The Office of Consumer Compliance monitors marketing and advertising on a continuous basis with a rule-set producing drift detection across the load-bearing rules of this policy: (a) pre-publication review coverage (100 percent of covered communications); (b) approval-version conformance (sampled and on-demand for any complaint or finding); (c) Reg DD Triggering Term and disclosure conformance (sampled monthly); (d) Reg Z open-end and closed-end Triggering Term and disclosure conformance (sampled monthly); (e) Reg E / 1005.18 prepaid pre-acquisition disclosure conformance (100 percent for active prepaid programs); (f) Reg B fair lending review completion for credit campaigns (100 percent); (g) FCRA Prescreened Solicitation firm-offer documentation, opt-out scrub, and short/long notice conformance (100 percent); (h) TCPA consent / EBR documentation, Do-Not-Call scrub cadence (no less than every 31 days for non-EBR campaigns), and calling-time conformance (100 percent for active campaigns); (i) CAN-SPAM compliance and 10-business-day opt-out honoring (sampled per send; 100 percent for opt-out honoring); (j) social media endorsement / Material Connection disclosure conformance (sampled monthly across active influencer engagements); (k) substantiation file presence for factual / comparative / ranking / rate claims (100 percent at approval); (l) advertising-related complaint trend monitoring under POL-COMP-001 with root-cause analysis where any single category exceeds 5 in any rolling 30-day window; (m) fair lending marketing-channel and target-list review under POL-FAIR-001 (quarterly).

Exceptions, root causes, and corrective actions are reported to the Consumer Compliance Committee on the Section 4 cadence. Independent testing is performed by Internal Audit on a risk-based cycle and by an independent third party as part of the Consumer Compliance independent-testing program no less frequently than every two years.

## 17. Training

Marketing Department personnel, contracted marketing-agency personnel with content-creation responsibilities, the Marketing Compliance Liaison, Compliance reviewers, telemarketing-vendor account managers, and email-service-provider account managers receive marketing-and-advertising compliance training within 30 days of role assignment and at least annually thereafter. Training covers UDAAP, Reg DD, Reg Z, Reg E and prepaid disclosures, Reg B, FCRA prescreening, TCPA / Do-Not-Call, CAN-SPAM, FFIEC social media guidance, and FTC endorsement guides, calibrated to the role. Training material is refreshed annually and promptly upon material regulatory change; unsatisfactory completion is escalated to the role's manager and to Compliance.

## 18. Violations and Enforcement

Violations -- distributing a covered communication without pre-publication approval, distributing an unapproved-version asset, omitting required disclosures, making unsubstantiated claims, failing to honor a Do-Not-Call or CAN-SPAM opt-out, or failing to disclose a Material Connection in an Endorsement -- are subject to corrective action up to and including termination of employment or vendor engagement, coordinated with Human Resources, the General Counsel, and (for vendors) POL-VRM-001. Suspected violations are reported promptly to the Office of Consumer Compliance and the Office of the General Counsel. Adverse external events (FTC or state-AG inquiry, CFPB Civil Investigative Demand, examination finding, class-action claim) are reported promptly to the General Counsel and the Board's Risk Committee. Consumer harm is remediated under POL-COMP-001 and POL-UDAAP-001.
