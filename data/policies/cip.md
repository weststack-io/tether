## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes how Meridian Trust Bank, N.A. ("the Bank") implements its Customer Identification Program ("CIP") in accordance with Section 326 of the Uniting and Strengthening America by Providing Appropriate Tools Required to Intercept and Obstruct Terrorism Act of 2001 (the "USA PATRIOT Act") and the joint CIP rule codified at 31 C.F.R. 1020.220 and reissued by each federal banking regulator at 12 C.F.R. 21.21 (OCC), 12 C.F.R. 208.63 (FRB), and 12 C.F.R. 326.8 (FDIC).

The CIP is a foundational element of the Bank's BSA/AML program (POL-BSA-001) and is designed to enable the Bank to form a reasonable belief that it knows the true identity of each Customer that opens an Account. The CIP supports the Bank's Customer Due Diligence ("CDD") obligations under 31 C.F.R. 1010.230, its Beneficial Ownership obligations for legal entity Customers, its OFAC sanctions screening obligations, and its Suspicious Activity reporting obligations.

CIP failures are reported to the BSA Officer, the Bank Secrecy Act / AML Compliance Committee, and the Board of Directors through the BSA program. CIP exceptions, lack-of-verification dispositions, and account-closure decisions feed the operational risk program, the consumer complaint program (POL-COMP-001), and the third-party risk program (POL-VRM-001) where the relevant onboarding channel is operated by or through a third party.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to every Account opened at the Bank for a Customer, in every channel (in-branch, online, mobile, telephone, mail, or through an authorized third-party introducer) and for every product (deposit, loan, safe deposit, fiduciary, treasury management, or other formal banking relationship). It applies to every employee, officer, contractor, and authorized third party that participates in account opening, identity verification, sanctions screening, recordkeeping, or related controls.

This policy supersedes any prior CIP policy. Where this policy conflicts with applicable law, regulation, or supervisory guidance, the law or guidance controls. Domain-specific policies (POL-BSA-001 BSA/AML; POL-VRM-001 vendor management; POL-IS-001 information security; POL-COMP-001 consumer complaint handling; the records retention policy) operate within this framework and inherit its definitions where consistent.

## 3. Definitions

**Account** has the meaning given in 31 C.F.R. 1020.100(a): a formal banking relationship established to provide or engage in services, dealings, or other financial transactions, including a deposit account, a transaction or asset account, a credit account, or other extension of credit. Account excludes a product or service for which a formal banking relationship is not established (e.g., the casual purchase of a check or wire transfer), an account opened to participate in an employee benefit plan established under ERISA, and any account that the Bank acquires through an acquisition, merger, purchase of assets, or assumption of liabilities.

**Customer** has the meaning given in 31 C.F.R. 1020.100(c): (i) a person that opens a new Account, and (ii) an individual who opens a new Account for another individual who lacks legal capacity, or for an entity that is not a legal person (such as a civic club). Customer does not include a financial institution regulated by a federal functional regulator, a bank regulated by a state bank regulator, a department or agency of the United States or a State, or a publicly traded company listed on a U.S. stock exchange (per 31 C.F.R. 1020.315(b)).

**U.S. Person** means a U.S. citizen or a person lawfully admitted for permanent residence in the United States, and an entity formed under the laws of the United States, any State, or the District of Columbia.

**Non-U.S. Person** means a Customer who is not a U.S. Person.

**Identifying Information** means, at a minimum, the four CIP elements required by 31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(2)(i) for every Customer: (a) name; (b) date of birth (for an individual); (c) address; and (d) identification number. For a U.S. Person, the identification number is a Taxpayer Identification Number (Social Security Number for an individual; Employer Identification Number for an entity). For a Non-U.S. Person, the identification number is one or more of: a Taxpayer Identification Number, a passport number and country of issuance, an alien identification card number, or a number and country of issuance of any other government-issued document evidencing nationality or residence and bearing a photograph or similar safeguard.

**Documentary Verification** means verifying a Customer's identity through documents, including: for an individual, an unexpired government-issued identification evidencing nationality or residence and bearing a photograph or similar safeguard (e.g., driver's license, state-issued ID card, passport); and for a person other than an individual, documents showing the existence of the entity (e.g., certified articles of incorporation, an unexpired government-issued business license, a partnership agreement, or a trust instrument).

**Non-Documentary Verification** means verifying a Customer's identity by means other than documents, including: contacting the Customer; independently verifying the Customer's identity through the comparison of information provided by the Customer with information obtained from a consumer reporting agency, a public database, or another source; checking references with other financial institutions; and obtaining a financial statement.

**Lack-of-Verification Procedures** means the procedures the Bank follows when it cannot form a reasonable belief that it knows the true identity of a Customer, as required by 31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(2)(iii), including the circumstances in which the Bank will not open an Account, the terms under which a Customer may use an Account while the Bank attempts to verify identity, the circumstances in which the Bank will close an Account after attempts to verify a Customer's identity have failed, and the circumstances in which the Bank will file a Suspicious Activity Report.

**Government List Comparison** means the determination required by 31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(4) of whether a Customer appears on any list of known or suspected terrorists or terrorist organizations issued by any federal government agency and designated as such by the U.S. Department of the Treasury in consultation with the federal functional regulators. Government List Comparison is operationally distinct from, but performed alongside, the OFAC sanctions screening required by 31 C.F.R. Chapter V.

**Beneficial Owner** has the meaning given in 31 C.F.R. 1010.230(d): each individual who, directly or indirectly, owns 25% or more of the equity interests of a legal entity Customer, and a single individual with significant responsibility to control, manage, or direct a legal entity Customer.

**Reliance** means the Bank's reliance on another financial institution to perform any procedure of this CIP, as permitted by 31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(6), where (i) such reliance is reasonable under the circumstances; (ii) the other financial institution is subject to a rule implementing 31 U.S.C. 5318(h) and is regulated by a federal functional regulator; and (iii) the other financial institution enters into a contract requiring it to certify annually that it has implemented its anti-money laundering program and will perform (or have its agent perform) the specified CIP requirements with respect to the Customer.

## 4. Governance and Roles

The BSA Officer owns this policy and the CIP, reports administratively to the Chief Risk Officer and functionally to the Board of Directors through the Bank Secrecy Act / AML Compliance Committee, and has independent authority to require an Account to remain unopened, to be restricted, or to be closed where CIP requirements cannot be met.

The Board of Directors approves the CIP as a part of POL-BSA-001 at least annually and receives quarterly reporting on CIP exception volume, lack-of-verification dispositions, third-party Reliance arrangements, Government List Comparison hits, customer-facing notice failures, and the results of independent testing.

Account-opening business lines (Retail Banking, Business Banking, Wealth Management, Treasury Management, Mortgage, and Indirect Lending) are accountable for executing CIP procedures at the point of sale and for routing exceptions, near-matches, and adverse-information findings to the BSA Officer's designee for disposition. Operations is accountable for retaining the records required by Section 11 of this policy. Information Technology is accountable for system controls that prevent activation of an Account before required CIP elements are captured and risk-rated.

## 5. Account Opening Procedures and Required Identifying Information

Before opening an Account for any Customer, the Bank must obtain the four CIP elements (name, date of birth where applicable, address, and identification number) defined in Section 3 of this policy. For an individual Customer, address must be a residential or business street address; an Army Post Office or Fleet Post Office number, the residential or business street address of next of kin or another contact individual, or a description of the physical location of the residence is acceptable only where an individual does not have a residential or business street address (31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(2)(i)(A)(3)). A post office box alone is not acceptable for an individual.

For a person other than an individual (a corporation, partnership, trust, limited liability company, or other legal entity), the Bank must obtain a principal place of business, local office, or other physical location.

For a Customer who has applied for, but not yet received, a Taxpayer Identification Number, the Bank may open the Account if (i) it confirms that the application was filed before the Customer opens the Account and (ii) it obtains the Taxpayer Identification Number within a reasonable period of time after the Account is opened (per 31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(2)(i)(B)). The Bank's standard "reasonable period of time" is 90 calendar days; any extension beyond 90 days requires BSA Officer approval and a documented basis.

Account-opening systems will not allow activation of an Account until each required CIP element has been captured, the Customer's identity has been verified or is on a documented and risk-accepted verification path, the Customer has been screened against the OFAC sanctions lists, and Government List Comparison has been performed.

## 6. Verification of Identifying Information

The Bank must verify enough of the Customer's Identifying Information to form a reasonable belief that it knows the true identity of the Customer. Verification must be completed within a reasonable time before or after the Account is opened (31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(2)(ii)). The Bank's standard "reasonable time" is at or before account opening for in-branch and remote channels where verification can be completed in real time, and within 30 calendar days after account opening where additional documentation must be requested. Any extension beyond 30 days requires risk-based approval by the BSA Officer's designee.

The Bank uses Documentary Verification, Non-Documentary Verification, or a combination of both, calibrated to the risk presented by the Customer, the type of Account, and the channel. Documentary Verification is preferred for in-branch openings of individual Accounts and for legal entity Accounts. Non-Documentary Verification is required, in addition to or in lieu of Documentary Verification, in the circumstances described in 31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(2)(ii)(B), including: the Customer is unable to present an unexpired government-issued identification document with photograph or similar safeguard; the Bank is not familiar with the documents presented; the Account is opened without obtaining documents; the Customer opens the Account without appearing in person; and other circumstances that increase the risk that the Bank will be unable to verify true identity.

For non-face-to-face account opening, the Bank performs at least one independent identity-data comparison against an authoritative third-party source (e.g., a knowledge-based authentication challenge derived from a consumer reporting agency, a device-and-document liveness check, or a digital identity attribute provider), in addition to capturing the four CIP elements, before activation.

For each Customer, the Bank documents the verification method used, the source(s) consulted, the result, the identity of the employee who performed the verification, and the date of verification.

## 7. Lack-of-Verification Procedures

When the Bank cannot form a reasonable belief that it knows the true identity of a Customer, it follows its Lack-of-Verification Procedures, as required by 31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(2)(iii):

(a) The Account is not opened, or, where the Account has already been opened pending verification, the Account is restricted (no withdrawals, no transfers out, no debit-card or check issuance, and no extensions of credit) pending resolution.

(b) Where verification cannot be completed within the timeframes in Section 6, the BSA Officer's designee determines whether to (i) extend the verification window with documented compensating controls, (ii) close the Account, or (iii) escalate for SAR consideration. A standing instruction to keep an unverified Account open absent documented BSA Officer approval is prohibited.

(c) Account closure pursuant to this Section is performed in accordance with the Bank's account-closure procedures and the consumer notice requirements applicable to the relevant deposit or credit product. Funds in a closed deposit Account are returned to the Customer unless OFAC blocking, legal process, or another lawful basis requires otherwise.

(d) The Bank determines whether to file a Suspicious Activity Report under 31 C.F.R. 1020.320 in light of the totality of the circumstances surrounding the Customer and the failed verification. Failure to verify identity, by itself, is not necessarily reportable; the determination requires evaluation of the indicia of attempted fraud, identity theft, or structuring.

The BSA Officer's designee reviews lack-of-verification dispositions monthly for consistency and documents trends in quarterly Board reporting.

## 8. Government List Comparison and OFAC Sanctions Screening

The Bank screens each Customer against the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List, the OFAC Consolidated Sanctions List, and any list of known or suspected terrorists or terrorist organizations issued by any federal government agency and designated by the Treasury in consultation with the federal functional regulators (31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(4)). Screening occurs at account opening, on each subsequent change to a Customer's Identifying Information, and on each update to a screened list. Beneficial Owners and authorized signers are screened on the same cadence.

A potential match is reviewed by the Office of Financial Crimes Compliance using a documented adjudication standard. Confirmed OFAC matches are blocked or rejected as required by 31 C.F.R. Chapter V and reported to OFAC within 10 business days of the blocking or rejected transaction. Confirmed Government List matches are referred immediately to the BSA Officer for any required notification, account treatment, and SAR consideration.

The Bank does not rely solely on a third-party screening vendor's "no hit" output. Vendor screening configurations (list scope, fuzzy-match threshold, refresh cadence, and adjudication workflow) are reviewed by the BSA Officer at least annually.

## 9. Customer Notice

The Bank provides notice to each Customer that information is being requested to verify the Customer's identity, in a manner reasonably designed to ensure that the Customer is able to view or otherwise receive the notice before the Account is opened (31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(5)). The standard notice reads: *"Important Information About Procedures for Opening a New Account. To help the government fight the funding of terrorism and money laundering activities, federal law requires all financial institutions to obtain, verify, and record information that identifies each person who opens an account. What this means for you: When you open an account, we will ask for your name, address, date of birth, and other information that will allow us to identify you. We may also ask to see your driver's license or other identifying documents."*

The notice is posted in branch lobbies, on the public sections of the Bank's website at the start of any online account-opening flow, in mobile account-opening flows, and on telephone and mail account-opening scripts. Marketing reviews any change to the notice for compliance with 31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(5) before publication.

## 10. Reliance on Another Financial Institution

The Bank may rely on another financial institution to perform any procedure of this CIP only where the conditions of 31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(6) are met: (i) reliance is reasonable under the circumstances; (ii) the other financial institution is subject to a rule implementing 31 U.S.C. 5318(h) and is regulated by a federal functional regulator; and (iii) the other financial institution enters into a written contract requiring it to certify annually that it has implemented its anti-money laundering program and will perform (or have its agent perform) the specified CIP requirements with respect to the Customer.

Reliance arrangements are approved in writing by the BSA Officer, governed under POL-VRM-001, and reviewed annually for current certifications and contractual compliance. The Bank retains responsibility for compliance with this CIP regardless of any Reliance arrangement.

## 11. Recordkeeping

The Bank retains, for each Customer, the following records (31 C.F.R. 1020.220(a)(3)):

(a) All Identifying Information obtained pursuant to Section 5, retained for five years after the date the Account is closed or, in the case of credit card accounts, five years after the Account is closed or becomes dormant.

(b) A description of any document relied on to verify identity, noting the type of document, any identification number contained in the document, the place of issuance, and, if any, the date of issuance and expiration date, retained for five years after the record is made.

(c) A description of the methods and the results of any measures undertaken to verify the identity of the Customer (including non-documentary methods, the source consulted, and the result), retained for five years after the record is made.

(d) A description of the resolution of any substantive discrepancy discovered when verifying the identifying information obtained, retained for five years after the record is made.

The Bank does not retain copies of identification documents as a substitute for capturing and retaining the descriptive information required above; the description controls. Where the Bank does retain a copy of an identification document, retention follows the records retention policy and applicable privacy and information security controls (POL-IS-001).

## 12. Compliance Monitoring

The BSA Officer's designee performs continuous compliance monitoring of the CIP to detect drift in any element of this policy, including: completeness of the four CIP elements at activation, appropriate use of Documentary versus Non-Documentary Verification given channel and risk, adherence to the 30-day verification window and the 90-day Taxpayer Identification Number window, lack-of-verification disposition consistency, OFAC and Government List comparison cadence and adjudication, customer-notice presence and currency in every channel, accuracy and completeness of the records required by Section 11, and the currency of contractual certifications under any Reliance arrangement.

CIP is a required scope element in the independent testing of the BSA/AML program performed under POL-BSA-001 (BSA/AML), and the results of independent testing are reported to the Bank Secrecy Act / AML Compliance Committee and the Board of Directors. Findings are tracked through the Bank's issue-management system with risk-based remediation timeframes.

## 13. Training

Every employee, officer, contractor, and authorized third-party introducer who participates in account opening, identity verification, sanctions screening, or related controls receives CIP training at hire and annually thereafter. Training is tailored to the employee's role and channel. Completion is tracked, and account-opening privileges are suspended for any employee with overdue training. The BSA Officer reviews training content annually for currency.

## 14. Violations and Enforcement

CIP failures, including failure to capture required Identifying Information, failure to perform verification within the required timeframes, failure to perform Government List Comparison or OFAC sanctions screening, failure to provide the required customer notice, and failure to retain the required records, are violations of this policy and may also be violations of federal law and regulation. Violations are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or removal of an authorized third party.

The Bank may face civil money penalties, regulatory enforcement actions, and reputational harm for systemic CIP failures. The BSA Officer reports any pattern or practice of CIP failures, and any individual failure that may rise to the level of a Suspicious Activity Report obligation, to the Bank Secrecy Act / AML Compliance Committee and to the Board of Directors.
