## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework by which Meridian Trust Bank, N.A. ("the Bank") complies with the Bank Secrecy Act (31 U.S.C. 5311 et seq.), the USA PATRIOT Act, and the implementing regulations of the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) at 31 C.F.R. Chapter X. The objective is to detect, deter, and report suspected money laundering, terrorist financing, and other illicit financial activity that may involve the Bank's products, services, customers, or geographies.

The policy is written to satisfy the four pillars of an AML program required by 31 C.F.R. 1020.210, plus the fifth pillar (customer due diligence and beneficial ownership) added in 2018:

1. A system of internal controls;
2. Independent testing of compliance;
3. A designated BSA compliance officer;
4. Ongoing personnel training; and
5. Risk-based customer due diligence and beneficial ownership identification.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all Bank employees, officers, directors, contractors, and third parties acting on the Bank's behalf, across every line of business, channel, and jurisdiction in which the Bank operates. It governs every product or service the Bank offers, including but not limited to demand deposit accounts, time deposits, consumer and commercial lending, treasury management, wire transfer, ACH origination, remote deposit capture, prepaid access, foreign correspondent banking relationships, and any new product introduced through the Bank's New Product Approval process.

Affiliates and operating subsidiaries are subject to this policy unless they have adopted an equivalent program approved in writing by the BSA Officer and the Bank's Board Risk Committee. Branch and agency operations of foreign banks operating in the United States that are part of the Bank's organizational chart are likewise covered.

This policy supersedes any prior BSA/AML policy. Where this policy conflicts with applicable law, the law controls; the BSA Officer must promptly update this policy to eliminate the conflict.

## 3. Definitions

**Beneficial Owner.** A natural person who, directly or indirectly, owns 25% or more of the equity interests of a legal entity customer, or a single individual with significant responsibility to control, manage, or direct that customer.

**Currency Transaction Report (CTR).** FinCEN Form 112 filed for each cash transaction in currency that exceeds $10,000 in a single business day, aggregating multiple transactions by or on behalf of the same person.

**Customer Due Diligence (CDD).** The collection and verification of information sufficient to understand the nature and purpose of customer relationships and to develop a customer risk profile.

**Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD).** Heightened CDD applied to higher-risk customers, including foreign politically exposed persons (PEPs), private banking accounts for non-U.S. persons subject to 31 U.S.C. 5318(i), and foreign correspondent banking relationships.

**Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC).** The U.S. Treasury office that administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions. While OFAC compliance is not technically part of the BSA, it is operationally integrated into this program.

**Politically Exposed Person (PEP).** A current or former senior foreign political figure, their immediate family, and close associates, as defined in 31 C.F.R. 1010.605.

**Suspicious Activity Report (SAR).** FinCEN Form 111 filed when the Bank knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect that a transaction involves funds derived from illegal activity, is designed to evade BSA requirements, has no apparent lawful purpose, or facilitates criminal activity, in any of the dollar thresholds set by 31 C.F.R. 1020.320.

**Structuring.** The act of breaking a transaction into smaller pieces to evade the CTR threshold or other reporting requirements, prohibited under 31 U.S.C. 5324.

## 4. Governance and the BSA Officer

The Board of Directors appoints a qualified BSA Compliance Officer who reports functionally to the Chief Compliance Officer and administratively to the Board Risk Committee. The BSA Officer has the authority, independence, and resources sufficient to administer the program and to escalate issues directly to the Board without filtering through line management.

The BSA Officer reports to the Board Risk Committee at least quarterly on:

- The volume and disposition of SAR filings, CTR filings, and 314(a)/314(b) information sharing;
- Open and aged suspicious activity investigations;
- Material findings from independent testing or examiner feedback;
- The status of remediation plans for any identified weaknesses;
- Changes to the BSA/AML risk assessment.

The Board annually approves this policy, the BSA/AML risk assessment, and the independent testing scope.

## 5. Risk Assessment

The Bank maintains a written enterprise BSA/AML risk assessment that is refreshed at least annually, or upon any material change to products, customers, geographies, services, or regulatory expectations. The assessment evaluates inherent risk factors including:

- Customer types (e.g., money services businesses, cash-intensive businesses, non-resident aliens, PEPs, private investment companies, charities, marijuana-related businesses, virtual asset service providers);
- Geographies (jurisdictions on FATF lists, OFAC sanctioned countries, HIDTA/HIFCA designated areas);
- Products and services (correspondent accounts, wires, prepaid, trade finance, private banking);
- Delivery channels (non-face-to-face onboarding, third-party introducers).

The assessment then evaluates the design and operating effectiveness of mitigating controls to derive a residual risk rating. Residual risk ratings drive the intensity of monitoring, the frequency of EDD refresh cycles, and the staffing of the Financial Crimes Compliance team.

## 6. Customer Identification Program (CIP)

The Bank's CIP is described in detail in POL-CIP-001 and is incorporated here by reference. At a minimum, for every new customer the Bank obtains and verifies name, date of birth (for individuals) or formation date (for entities), residential or business address, and an identifying number (TIN, EIN, or, for foreign nationals, passport or other government-issued ID). The Bank does not open an account before CIP verification is complete except in narrow exception circumstances documented and approved by the BSA Officer.

## 7. Customer Due Diligence and Beneficial Ownership

For each legal entity customer opening a new account on or after May 11, 2018, the Bank collects and verifies the identity of each beneficial owner under the ownership prong (25% threshold) and the control prong (one individual with significant managerial control). The Bank uses FinCEN's Certification of Beneficial Owner(s) form, or a substantively equivalent internal form, and refreshes the certification when the Bank becomes aware of a change.

For all customers, the Bank establishes the nature and expected purpose of the relationship at onboarding and develops a customer risk profile. CDD information is refreshed on a risk-based cadence: low-risk customers at least every 36 months, medium-risk at least every 24 months, and high-risk at least every 12 months, or sooner upon the occurrence of a trigger event such as a SAR filing, OFAC match, negative news hit, or significant change in transactional behavior.

## 8. Enhanced Due Diligence

EDD is applied to relationships rated high risk by the customer risk model and to specific categories required by law:

- Foreign correspondent accounts (31 U.S.C. 5318(j) and 5318(k));
- Private banking accounts for non-U.S. persons (31 U.S.C. 5318(i));
- Foreign PEPs and their immediate family / close associates;
- Money services businesses;
- Virtual asset service providers and other digital asset intermediaries;
- Customers operating in jurisdictions identified by FATF as having strategic deficiencies.

EDD includes obtaining additional information about the source of wealth, source of funds, expected activity, and beneficial ownership beyond the 25% threshold where appropriate, and ongoing monitoring at heightened sensitivity.

## 9. Suspicious Activity Monitoring and Reporting

The Bank deploys an automated transaction monitoring system that applies rules, scenarios, and behavioral analytics across deposit, lending, wire, ACH, and card activity to surface unusual patterns. Alerts are triaged by Level 1 analysts within five business days of generation; cases that warrant further review are escalated to Level 2 investigators.

A SAR is filed within 30 calendar days of the date of initial detection of facts that may constitute a basis for filing. If no suspect is identified on the date of detection, the Bank may delay filing for an additional 30 calendar days, but in no case may the period exceed 60 calendar days from initial detection. Continuing activity SARs are filed every 90 days as required by 31 C.F.R. 1020.320(b)(3).

SAR filing decisions are documented in the case management system. The Bank does not disclose the existence or content of a SAR to any person involved in the transaction, in accordance with 31 U.S.C. 5318(g)(2).

## 10. Currency Transaction Reporting

The Bank files a CTR for each cash transaction or aggregated set of transactions in currency exceeding $10,000 conducted by, through, or on behalf of the same person in a single business day. CTRs are filed within 15 calendar days of the transaction date. The Bank applies CTR aggregation logic across branches and channels and prohibits employees from helping a customer structure a transaction to avoid the CTR threshold.

## 11. OFAC Sanctions Screening

All customers, beneficial owners, related parties, and counterparty wire/ACH transaction parties are screened against OFAC's Specially Designated Nationals List, Sectoral Sanctions Identifications List, and applicable consolidated sanctions lists at onboarding, daily, and on every payment. Apparent matches are reviewed by the Sanctions team, blocked or rejected as appropriate, and reported to OFAC within 10 business days of the blocking action.

## 12. Information Sharing -- 314(a) and 314(b)

The Bank designates a 314(a) point of contact with FinCEN and processes 314(a) requests within 14 days of receipt. The Bank participates in voluntary 314(b) information sharing with other financial institutions for the purpose of identifying and reporting money laundering and terrorist financing, after filing the requisite notice with FinCEN and confirming the counterparty's active 314(b) status.

## 13. Training

All employees, officers, and directors receive BSA/AML training appropriate to their role. New hires complete training within 30 days of start. All covered personnel complete refresher training at least annually. Specialized training is delivered to high-touch roles (tellers, branch managers, BSA analysts, lending officers, wire operations) on a more frequent cadence. Training completion is tracked and reported to the Board Risk Committee.

## 14. Independent Testing

Independent testing of the BSA/AML program is performed at least every 12 to 18 months by Internal Audit or by a qualified external firm. The scope covers the design and operating effectiveness of the four pillars plus CDD/beneficial ownership. Findings are tracked to remediation in the enterprise issues management system, with the BSA Officer accountable for closure within agreed-upon timelines.

## 15. Recordkeeping

The Bank retains all CIP, CDD, beneficial ownership, SAR, CTR, OFAC, and 314(a)/314(b) records for at least five years after the account is closed or the report is filed, in accordance with 31 C.F.R. 1010.430. Records are retained in a manner that permits prompt retrieval for examiner or law enforcement requests.

## 16. Compliance Monitoring

The Financial Crimes Compliance team performs ongoing quality assurance over alert disposition, SAR narratives, CTR accuracy, and CDD refresh timeliness. Sampling rates are calibrated to risk and adjusted based on prior QA findings. Quarterly metrics are reported to the BSA Officer and to the Board Risk Committee.

## 17. Violations and Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy is grounds for disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may also expose the responsible individual to civil money penalties or criminal liability under 31 U.S.C. 5321 and 5322. Any employee who suspects a violation of this policy or applicable BSA/AML law must report it promptly to the BSA Officer or through the Bank's confidential ethics hotline. Retaliation against good-faith reporters is strictly prohibited.
