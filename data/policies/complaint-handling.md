## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes how Meridian Trust Bank, N.A. ("the Bank") receives, acknowledges, investigates, resolves, and reports consumer complaints. A robust complaint handling program protects consumers from harm, supports compliance with the federal consumer financial laws administered by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) and the Bank's prudential regulator, and surfaces operational, product, and conduct issues that warrant root cause remediation.

Complaint data is treated as a primary input to the Bank's Compliance Management System (CMS). Trends identified through this program feed the annual Compliance Risk Assessment, the Fair Lending review, the UDAAP monitoring program, and product-level governance.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to every line of business, channel, and product offered by the Bank, including consumer deposits, consumer lending (residential mortgage, home equity, auto, unsecured installment, credit card), small business products held by natural persons, treasury management services used by sole proprietors, prepaid access, and digital and mobile banking. It also covers complaints touching third-party service providers acting on the Bank's behalf and complaints related to debt collection of the Bank's own portfolio.

The policy applies to all employees, officers, directors, and contractors. Affiliates and operating subsidiaries are subject to this policy unless they have adopted an equivalent program approved in writing by the Director of Consumer Compliance and the Bank's Board Risk Committee.

This policy supersedes any prior consumer complaint handling policy. Where this policy conflicts with applicable law or a binding regulatory order, the law or order controls; the Director of Consumer Compliance must promptly update this policy to eliminate the conflict.

## 3. Definitions

**Complaint.** Any expression of dissatisfaction submitted to the Bank by, or on behalf of, a consumer regarding a Bank product, service, employee, agent, or third-party provider, regardless of channel or whether the consumer requests a specific remedy. Inquiries that can be resolved at first contact without further investigation are not complaints; if any investigation, escalation, or written response is required, the matter is a complaint.

**Regulatory Complaint.** A complaint received through a regulator, including but not limited to the CFPB Consumer Response portal, a state attorney general, a state banking department, the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC), the Federal Reserve, the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC), or the Better Business Bureau when forwarded by a regulator.

**Executive Complaint.** A complaint addressed to a member of executive management, the Board of Directors, or filed in litigation or arbitration. Executive complaints follow the same investigation workflow as standard complaints but with mandatory legal review.

**UDAAP.** Unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices, as defined in 12 U.S.C. 5531 and 5536 and CFPB supervisory guidance.

**Root Cause.** The underlying systemic, procedural, training, technology, vendor, product, or governance failure that produced or permitted the complained-of conduct. A root cause is not the proximate action of an individual employee unless the analysis demonstrates that the action was not foreseeable given existing controls.

**Acknowledgment.** A communication to the complainant confirming receipt of the complaint, identifying the matter by reference number, and stating the expected timeframe for substantive response.

**Substantive Response.** A written or verbal communication addressing the substance of the complaint, including the Bank's findings, the resolution offered, and information about any further dispute rights the consumer may have.

## 4. Channels of Receipt

The Bank accepts complaints through every channel by which it accepts customer communications, without exception. Designated channels include:

- The Bank's general toll-free telephone line and any product-specific service lines;
- Branch personnel, who must capture in-person and telephone complaints in the complaint system before end of business the same day;
- The Bank's secure messaging facility within online and mobile banking;
- Inbound postal mail addressed to any Bank office;
- Email to consumer-facing addresses including the addresses published on the Bank's website and on adverse action notices, billing statements, and disclosure documents;
- Social media accounts operated by the Bank, with public posts captured by the Bank's social media monitoring vendor and routed to Consumer Compliance within four hours of receipt;
- Regulator portals, including but not limited to the CFPB Consumer Response portal;
- Litigation and arbitration filings, captured by the Office of General Counsel and routed to Consumer Compliance.

Employees may not direct a consumer to a different channel as a precondition for receiving the complaint. Refusing to capture a complaint, or coaching a consumer to withdraw or restate a complaint as something other than a complaint, is a serious violation of this policy and is subject to discipline up to and including termination.

## 5. Intake and Acknowledgment

Every complaint must be entered into the Bank's centralized complaint management system ("the System") by the receiving employee or by a designated intake team within one business day of receipt. The intake record must capture, at minimum:

- The consumer's name and preferred contact information;
- The product or service involved;
- The channel through which the complaint was received;
- The verbatim text or summary of the complaint;
- Any documents provided by the consumer;
- The date and time of receipt and the receiving employee's identifier.

The System assigns a unique reference number and routes the matter to the appropriate business unit for investigation. The Bank will issue an acknowledgment to the consumer within five business days of receipt for standard complaints, and within the timeframe required by applicable regulation for matters subject to a specific deadline (for example, billing error notices under Regulation Z and electronic fund transfer error claims under Regulation E).

## 6. Investigation and Resolution

The business unit assigned to a complaint must complete a substantive response within thirty calendar days of receipt unless an applicable regulation specifies a shorter or longer period, in which case the regulatory deadline controls. Regulatory complaints received through the CFPB Consumer Response portal must receive a substantive response within fifteen calendar days, with closure within sixty calendar days, in alignment with the CFPB's published company response expectations.

Investigation must include, at minimum:

- Review of the customer's account history, transaction records, and prior contacts with the Bank;
- Review of the policies, procedures, disclosures, and any model marketing or servicing material in effect at the time of the complained-of conduct;
- Interview of any employee identified by the consumer or by the System's contact records;
- Review of relevant call recordings, secure message threads, and branch security footage where available;
- For complaints involving a third-party service provider, request and review of the vendor's records and any explanation of the conduct.

Resolution may take the form of an explanation, an apology, restitution of fees, refund of interest or principal, correction of credit reporting, account adjustment, replacement of a card or document, change to a process or training, or any combination of these. The Bank does not require a consumer to release legal claims as a condition of receiving restitution that the Bank determines is owed under the Bank's policies, applicable law, or a regulatory expectation. Releases may be requested only where authorized by the Office of General Counsel.

Substantive responses must be in plain language, must reference the matter by its System number, must identify the products and services at issue, must state the Bank's findings and the resolution offered, and must inform the consumer of any further dispute rights, including the right to contact the Bank's regulator. For matters that resulted in a credit reporting dispute under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, the response must comply with the FCRA's consumer notice requirements.

## 7. Root Cause Analysis

Every complaint closed in the System is assigned a root cause code from the Bank's standardized root cause taxonomy, which includes categories such as Disclosure Defect, Training Gap, System Defect, Vendor Performance, Sales Conduct, Servicing Conduct, Collections Conduct, Fee Practice, Marketing Practice, Account Documentation, and Fraud (Bank-Caused vs. Third-Party).

Consumer Compliance reviews root cause assignments monthly. Any root cause category that exceeds the Bank's threshold for trend escalation, or any individual complaint that surfaces a possible UDAAP issue, fair lending issue, or pattern affecting more than fifty consumers, is escalated to the Compliance Issues Committee for formal issue management under the Bank's Issues and Findings policy.

Root cause findings that point to a defect in a policy, procedure, training program, system, vendor agreement, or product feature must be referred to the relevant control owner with a remediation due date. Consumer Compliance tracks remediation through closure and reports outstanding remediation items to the Board Risk Committee quarterly.

## 8. Restitution

Where the Bank determines that a consumer was charged a fee that was not earned, was charged interest in excess of the contract rate, was denied a benefit to which the consumer was entitled, or was otherwise harmed by the Bank's conduct, the Bank will provide restitution. Restitution amounts are calculated to make the consumer whole, including any out-of-pocket costs reasonably foreseeable as a result of the conduct, and including interest on monetary restitution at the Bank's posted savings rate from the date of harm to the date of payment.

Where the Bank identifies a pattern of conduct that affected consumers who have not complained, the Bank will conduct lookback restitution to all affected consumers. The Bank does not require the affected consumer to have complained as a precondition for restitution under a lookback.

Restitution above the dollar threshold set in the Bank's Delegations of Authority requires approval of the Compliance Issues Committee. Restitution above the executive threshold requires approval of the Board Risk Committee.

## 9. Recordkeeping

Complaint records, including the original communication, all investigation work papers, the substantive response, the root cause assignment, and any restitution records, are retained in the System for the longer of seven years from closure or the period required by applicable law. Records relating to a regulatory examination or supervisory matter are retained until the Bank's General Counsel and Compliance functions confirm in writing that retention is no longer required.

## 10. Reporting

Consumer Compliance produces, at minimum, the following reports:

- A monthly Complaint Trend Report distributed to each line of business, identifying volume, root cause distribution, average response time, restitution paid, and outliers requiring remediation;
- A monthly Regulatory Complaint Report identifying every CFPB Consumer Response matter and every state regulator complaint, with status and aging;
- A quarterly Complaint Risk Report to the Board Risk Committee, integrating complaint trends with the Fair Lending review, the UDAAP monitoring program, and the Compliance Risk Assessment;
- An annual Complaint Program Effectiveness Review, presented to the Board Risk Committee, evaluating the program's coverage, timeliness, root cause discipline, and remediation closure rate.

## 11. Compliance Monitoring

Consumer Compliance conducts quality assurance reviews of a statistically valid sample of closed complaints each quarter. Reviews assess intake completeness, response timeliness, response quality, root cause accuracy, and restitution adequacy. Findings are reported to the responsible business unit leader and tracked to remediation. The Bank's Internal Audit function performs an independent review of the complaint program no less than every eighteen months.

The Bank's complaint volumes, trends, and program effectiveness metrics are compared against peer institutions using publicly available CFPB Consumer Response data and any peer benchmarking the Bank lawfully obtains.

## 12. Violations and Enforcement

Failure to capture a complaint, intentional misclassification of a complaint as an inquiry, retaliation against a consumer for filing a complaint, retaliation against an employee for escalating a complaint, or interference with a regulator's review of complaint handling is a violation of this policy. Violations are subject to discipline up to and including termination, and may be referred to law enforcement or the Bank's regulator where required by law.

Material violations, including any pattern of failure to capture or respond to complaints, must be reported by the Director of Consumer Compliance to the Bank's General Counsel and the Chair of the Board Risk Committee within five business days of identification.
