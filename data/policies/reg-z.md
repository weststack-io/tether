## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes how Meridian Trust Bank, N.A. ("the Bank") complies with the Truth in Lending Act (TILA), 15 U.S.C. 1601 et seq., and its implementing Regulation Z, 12 C.F.R. Part 1026, which is administered by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB). Regulation Z governs disclosures, calculations, advertising, and substantive consumer protections for consumer credit, including closed-end residential mortgage loans subject to the integrated TILA-RESPA Disclosure (TRID) rule, home-equity lines of credit (HELOCs), open-end credit (including credit cards subject to the Credit CARD Act of 2009), and certain high-cost mortgages under the Home Ownership and Equity Protection Act (HOEPA).

The policy is a primary input to the Bank's Compliance Management System (CMS). APR and finance-charge errors, redisclosure failures, rescission failures, prohibited credit-card practices, and misleading advertisements identified under this policy feed the consumer complaint program (POL-COMP-001), the fair lending program (POL-FL-001), the operational risk program, and the UDAAP monitoring program (12 U.S.C. 5531 / 5536).

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to every offer, application, origination, modification, servicing, advertisement, or marketing communication for consumer credit extended by the Bank. "Consumer credit" means credit extended to a natural person primarily for personal, family, or household purposes within the meaning of 12 C.F.R. 1026.2(a)(12). Covered products include closed-end residential mortgage loans, home-equity lines of credit, consumer-purpose closed-end personal loans, indirect motor vehicle loans purchased from dealers, and consumer credit cards.

The policy applies to every employee, officer, director, contractor, and third-party service provider that originates, advertises, discloses, prices, services, or collects on consumer credit on the Bank's behalf, including third-party loan originators, settlement agents, and the Bank's credit-card servicer. Business-purpose credit, agricultural credit, credit extended to non-natural persons, and credit above the annually adjusted Regulation Z threshold for non-real-estate-secured credit are out of scope.

This policy supersedes any prior Regulation Z policy. Where this policy conflicts with applicable law, regulation, or supervisory guidance, the law or guidance controls.

## 3. Definitions

**Annual Percentage Rate (APR).** The cost of consumer credit expressed as a yearly rate, calculated under 12 C.F.R. 1026.22 for closed-end credit and 12 C.F.R. 1026.14 for open-end credit. The APR includes the interest rate plus prepaid finance charges expressed as a single yearly rate using the actuarial method or, for closed-end credit, the U.S. Rule.

**Finance Charge.** The cost of consumer credit as a dollar amount, including any charge payable directly or indirectly by the consumer and imposed directly or indirectly by the creditor as an incident to or a condition of the extension of credit, as set out in 12 C.F.R. 1026.4(a). Examples in 1026.4(b) include interest, points, loan-origination fees, mortgage broker fees, premium for required credit insurance, and certain third-party charges. Exclusions are listed in 1026.4(c).

**APR Tolerance.** The permissible deviation between the disclosed APR and the actual APR. Under 12 C.F.R. 1026.22(a)(2), the regular tolerance is 1/8 of one percentage point for regular transactions and 1/4 of one percentage point for irregular transactions. For TRID-covered transactions, additional tolerances under 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(f) apply at consummation.

**Loan Estimate (LE).** The integrated disclosure required by 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(e) for most closed-end residential mortgage transactions, delivered or placed in the mail no later than the third business day after the Bank receives the consumer's application as defined in 12 C.F.R. 1026.2(a)(3) (six elements: name, income, Social Security number, property address, estimated property value, and mortgage loan amount sought).

**Closing Disclosure (CD).** The integrated disclosure required by 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(f), which the consumer must receive no later than three business days before consummation of a TRID-covered loan. Certain changes after the initial CD trigger a new three-business-day waiting period under 1026.19(f)(2)(ii) (APR change beyond tolerance, addition of a prepayment penalty, change in loan product).

**Right of Rescission.** The consumer's right under 15 U.S.C. 1635 and 12 C.F.R. 1026.23 to rescind a non-purchase-money credit transaction secured by the consumer's principal dwelling within three business days of the later of consummation, delivery of the material disclosures, or delivery of two copies of the rescission notice.

**High-Cost Mortgage (HOEPA).** A consumer-purpose closed-end or open-end credit transaction secured by the consumer's principal dwelling that exceeds any of the APR, points-and-fees, or prepayment-penalty triggers in 12 C.F.R. 1026.32(a). HOEPA loans carry additional restrictions under 1026.34.

**Higher-Priced Mortgage Loan (HPML).** A closed-end consumer credit transaction secured by the consumer's principal dwelling whose APR exceeds the Average Prime Offer Rate (APOR) by 1.5 percentage points (first lien), 2.5 percentage points (jumbo first lien), or 3.5 percentage points (subordinate lien), as defined in 12 C.F.R. 1026.35(a).

**Ability-to-Repay (ATR).** The Bank's obligation under 12 C.F.R. 1026.43(c) to make a reasonable, good-faith determination at or before consummation that the consumer will have a reasonable ability to repay a covered closed-end residential mortgage loan. Qualified Mortgages under 12 C.F.R. 1026.43(e) carry a presumption of compliance.

**Billing Error.** Any of the categories defined in 12 C.F.R. 1026.13(a) for open-end credit, including unauthorized use, transactions not made by the consumer, computational errors, failure to credit payments, and requests for documentary evidence.

## 4. Application, Loan Estimate, and TRID Disclosure Timing

For every closed-end consumer credit transaction secured by real property or a cooperative unit and not otherwise exempt under 12 C.F.R. 1026.3, the Bank delivers or places in the mail a Loan Estimate not later than the third business day after receipt of an application meeting the six-element definition in 12 C.F.R. 1026.2(a)(3), and not later than the seventh business day before consummation under 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(e)(1)(iii). The "business day" used for the seven-day rule is the broader definition in 12 C.F.R. 1026.2(a)(6) (all calendar days except Sundays and the federal legal holidays in 5 U.S.C. 6103(a)).

The Bank does not require the consumer to provide any document verifying information before delivering the Loan Estimate. The Bank may not impose any fee in connection with the application before the consumer has received the Loan Estimate and indicated an intent to proceed, except a bona fide and reasonable credit-report fee (12 C.F.R. 1026.19(e)(2)(i)). The Bank documents the consumer's intent to proceed and the date and method of Loan Estimate delivery for every file.

Revised Loan Estimates are issued only when a valid changed-circumstance basis under 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(e)(3)(iv) exists. The Bank issues the revised Loan Estimate within three business days of receiving information establishing the changed circumstance and not later than four business days before consummation. The basis for every revised Loan Estimate is documented in the loan file.

## 5. Closing Disclosure and the Three-Business-Day Waiting Period

The Bank ensures the consumer receives the Closing Disclosure no later than three business days before consummation under 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(f)(1)(ii). Receipt is presumed three business days after mailing unless the Bank has evidence of earlier receipt. The Bank prefers electronic delivery with E-SIGN consent and tracked acknowledgment.

A new three-business-day waiting period is required, and a revised Closing Disclosure must be provided, when any of the three triggers in 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(f)(2)(ii) occurs after the initial Closing Disclosure: (1) the disclosed APR becomes inaccurate beyond the tolerance in 12 C.F.R. 1026.22, (2) a prepayment penalty is added, or (3) the loan product changes (e.g., from fixed to adjustable rate). Other post-CD changes require a corrected Closing Disclosure but not a new waiting period.

The Bank does not waive or shorten the three-business-day waiting period except upon the consumer's written modification based on a bona fide personal financial emergency under 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(f)(1)(iv). The waiver must describe the emergency, specifically modify or waive the waiting period, and bear the signature of all consumers who are primarily liable. Pre-printed or template waiver forms are prohibited.

## 6. Finance Charge and APR Accuracy

The Bank computes the finance charge by aggregating every charge required by 12 C.F.R. 1026.4(a) and (b) and excluding only those charges expressly excluded by 12 C.F.R. 1026.4(c) through (e). Underwriting and disclosure systems are configured to default to inclusion in the finance charge when the treatment of a charge is ambiguous; the Lending Compliance Director must approve any exclusion of a non-routine fee.

For closed-end credit, the disclosed APR must be calculated under 12 C.F.R. 1026.22 and Appendix J. The disclosed APR is considered accurate if it does not vary from the actual APR by more than 1/8 of one percentage point for regular transactions or 1/4 of one percentage point for irregular transactions (12 C.F.R. 1026.22(a)(2)). For mortgage transactions secured by real property, an understated finance charge is also considered accurate if the disclosed amount is no more than $100 below the actual finance charge (12 C.F.R. 1026.18(d)(1) for non-TRID mortgages) or within the integrated tolerance rules in 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(f) for TRID-covered transactions. Any APR or finance-charge variance exceeding tolerance is reported to the Lending Compliance Director within one business day for restitution analysis under 12 C.F.R. 1026.18(d) Note and, where applicable, the TILA reimbursement framework administered by the prudential regulators.

## 7. Right of Rescission

For every consumer credit transaction in which a security interest is or will be retained or acquired in the consumer's principal dwelling, other than a residential mortgage transaction (i.e., a purchase-money transaction excluded under 12 C.F.R. 1026.23(f)), the Bank delivers two copies of the notice of right to rescind to each consumer entitled to rescind, together with the material disclosures, before consummation. The notice must clearly and conspicuously disclose the retention or acquisition of a security interest in the consumer's principal dwelling, the consumer's right to rescind, how to exercise it, the effects of rescission, and the date the rescission period expires (12 C.F.R. 1026.23(b)).

The rescission period runs until midnight of the third business day after the later of consummation, delivery of the rescission notice, or delivery of all material disclosures. "Business day" for rescission means all calendar days except Sundays and the federal legal holidays specified in 5 U.S.C. 6103(a). If the Bank fails to deliver the required notice or material disclosures, the rescission period extends up to three years under 15 U.S.C. 1635(f).

If a consumer rescinds, the Bank returns within 20 calendar days any money or property given as earnest money, downpayment, or otherwise, and takes any action necessary to terminate the security interest (12 C.F.R. 1026.23(d)(2)). The Bank does not disburse any funds, perform any services, or deliver any materials secured by the dwelling until the rescission period has expired and the Bank has reasonably satisfied itself that the consumer has not rescinded.

## 8. HOEPA, HPML, and Ability-to-Repay

Before consummating a closed-end consumer credit transaction secured by the consumer's principal dwelling, the Bank evaluates the loan against the high-cost mortgage triggers in 12 C.F.R. 1026.32(a) (APR trigger, points-and-fees trigger, and prepayment-penalty trigger). If any trigger is met, the loan is a HOEPA loan and the Bank applies the additional disclosure (1026.32(c)), counseling (1026.34(a)(5)), and substantive restrictions in 12 C.F.R. 1026.32 and 1026.34, including the prohibitions on balloon payments, prepayment penalties, and loan flipping. The Bank does not originate HOEPA loans except in accordance with a written exception approved by the Lending Compliance Director and Senior Credit Officer.

For every HPML covered by 12 C.F.R. 1026.35, the Bank establishes and maintains an escrow account for at least the first five years for property taxes and required insurance unless an exemption applies, obtains a written interior appraisal that complies with 12 C.F.R. 1026.35(c), and provides the consumer with a copy of the appraisal at least three business days before consummation.

The Bank makes a reasonable and good-faith determination at or before consummation that every consumer has a reasonable ability to repay the loan according to its terms, considering at minimum the eight underwriting factors in 12 C.F.R. 1026.43(c)(2) (current or reasonably expected income or assets, current employment status, monthly payment on the covered transaction, monthly payment on any simultaneous loan, monthly payment for mortgage-related obligations, current debt obligations and alimony or child support, monthly debt-to-income ratio or residual income, and credit history). The Bank verifies each of these factors using third-party records described in 12 C.F.R. 1026.43(c)(4). Loans originated as Qualified Mortgages must satisfy the applicable QM definition in 12 C.F.R. 1026.43(e).

## 9. Open-End Credit and Credit Cards

For every open-end consumer credit account, the Bank provides the account-opening disclosures required by 12 C.F.R. 1026.6 before the first transaction, periodic statements that comply with 12 C.F.R. 1026.7, and change-in-terms notices that comply with 12 C.F.R. 1026.9 not less than 45 days before the change becomes effective for credit cards (12 C.F.R. 1026.9(c)(2)(i)). Penalty rates and significant changes in terms may not be applied to existing balances except as permitted by the limited exceptions in 12 C.F.R. 1026.55.

For credit-card accounts, the Bank does not open an account or increase any credit limit without considering the consumer's ability to make required minimum payments under 12 C.F.R. 1026.51, including the additional limitations in 12 C.F.R. 1026.51(b) for consumers under 21. The Bank does not assess any fee or charge that is not reasonable and proportional to the violation of account terms (12 C.F.R. 1026.52(b)). The safe-harbor amounts in 1026.52(b)(1)(ii) are reviewed annually for CFPB-published adjustments.

The Bank does not assess over-the-credit-limit fees unless the consumer has affirmatively consented to authorize over-the-credit-limit transactions (12 C.F.R. 1026.56). Default opt-in and pre-checked consent are prohibited. Payments received by the cutoff time on the due date are credited as of the date received under 12 C.F.R. 1026.10(a), and any payment in excess of the minimum payment is allocated first to the balance with the highest APR under 12 C.F.R. 1026.53.

For billing errors asserted in writing within 60 days after the Bank transmitted the periodic statement, the Bank acknowledges in writing within 30 days and resolves the dispute within two complete billing cycles (and in any event not more than 90 days) under 12 C.F.R. 1026.13. During the investigation, the Bank does not collect the disputed amount, assess finance charges or other fees on it, restrict or close the account, or report the consumer as delinquent on the disputed amount.

## 10. Advertising

Every advertisement for consumer credit subject to Regulation Z is reviewed for compliance with the actually-available, clear-and-conspicuous, and triggering-term requirements in 12 C.F.R. 1026.16 (open-end) and 12 C.F.R. 1026.24 (closed-end). For closed-end credit, the Bank does not advertise a rate of finance charge unless the rate is stated as an annual percentage rate using that term, and where the APR may be increased after consummation, the advertisement so states (1026.24(c)). When an advertisement states any of the triggering terms in 1026.24(d)(1), all required additional disclosures in 1026.24(d)(2) must appear with equal prominence.

The Bank prohibits the seven advertising practices specified in 12 C.F.R. 1026.24(i) for credit secured by a dwelling, including misleading use of "fixed," misleading comparisons of credit terms, misrepresentation of government endorsement, misleading use of the consumer's lender's name, misleading claims of debt elimination, misleading use of "counselor," and misleading foreign-language advertisements. Marketing is responsible for first-line review; Compliance must clear every advertisement for closed-end credit secured by a dwelling, every credit card solicitation, and every advertisement that uses a triggering term, before publication. Approval records are retained for at least 24 months under 12 C.F.R. 1026.25.

## 11. Recordkeeping

The Bank retains evidence of compliance with Regulation Z for at least two years after the disclosures are required to be made (12 C.F.R. 1026.25(a)). For closed-end mortgage transactions, the Bank retains the Loan Estimate, Closing Disclosure, evidence of compliance with 12 C.F.R. 1026.19(e) and (f), and ATR/QM documentation for at least five years after consummation under 12 C.F.R. 1026.25(c). Records under the loan-originator-compensation rule (12 C.F.R. 1026.36) are retained for at least three years.

## 12. Compliance Monitoring

The Lending Compliance Director maintains a continuous monitoring program covering, at a minimum: (a) APR and finance-charge accuracy testing on a statistically valid quarterly sample of closed-end loans, with any tolerance variance escalated within one business day; (b) timing testing for the Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure (including the post-CD waiting-period triggers); (c) rescission notice content, delivery, and tracking; (d) HOEPA trigger evaluation on every dwelling-secured closed-end loan; (e) HPML escrow, appraisal, and disclosure compliance; (f) ATR / QM file documentation; (g) credit-card change-in-terms notice timing, penalty-fee reasonableness and proportionality, payment allocation, and billing-error procedure timing; and (h) advertising review approvals and the seven prohibited dwelling-secured practices in 1026.24(i). Findings are reported monthly to the Compliance Committee and quarterly to the Risk Committee of the Board.

## 13. Training

Every employee, officer, and director who originates, processes, underwrites, prices, services, or markets consumer credit completes Regulation Z training before assuming Regulation Z responsibilities and at least annually thereafter. Training covers rule changes in the prior twelve months, the Bank's procedures, common error patterns identified in monitoring, and any consent orders or supervisory findings issued by the CFPB or the prudential regulators.

## 14. Violations and Enforcement

A confirmed violation of this policy is investigated under the Bank's Compliance Investigations Procedure (POL-COMPLINV-001). Consumer harm is remediated promptly through restitution, credit-bureau correction, fee refunds, rate adjustments, escrow corrections, and any other remedy required to make the consumer whole. The Bank does not condition restitution on the consumer's release of claims that the Bank concludes are owed. Material violations and any pattern or practice of violations are reported to the Bank's primary federal regulator and, where required, to the CFPB and the affected consumers, on the timelines required by applicable law.

Employee discipline for Regulation Z violations follows the Bank's progressive discipline framework. Third-party loan originators and service providers that fail to comply are subject to remediation, contractual cure, financial offset, or termination under POL-VENDOR-001.
