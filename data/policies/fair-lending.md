## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework by which Meridian Trust Bank, N.A. ("the Bank") prevents, detects, and remediates illegal discrimination in credit transactions. Fair lending compliance is a core obligation of the Bank under the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and Regulation B (12 C.F.R. Part 1002), the Fair Housing Act (FHA, 42 U.S.C. 3601 et seq.), the Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA) and Regulation C (12 C.F.R. Part 1003), the Community Reinvestment Act (CRA) where applicable, and the prohibitions on unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices (UDAAP) under 12 U.S.C. 5531 and 5536 to the extent UDAAP overlaps with disparate treatment or impact in credit.

The Bank's fair lending program is designed to identify disparate treatment, disparate impact, redlining, steering, and underwriting or pricing variation that cannot be explained by legitimate, documented, business-justified factors, and to remediate consumers who were harmed.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to every consumer and small business credit product offered by the Bank, including residential mortgage origination and servicing, home equity lines and loans, auto and other secured installment lending, unsecured installment lending, credit cards, overdraft lines of credit, and small business loans where the applicant or borrower is a natural person or a single-member entity. It applies in every channel, including in-branch, broker, correspondent, indirect dealer, online, mobile, and call-center.

This policy applies to all employees, officers, directors, and contractors of the Bank, and to vendors and third-party originators acting on the Bank's behalf. The Bank requires its third-party originators and indirect dealer partners to comply with this policy through contract terms enforceable by the Bank.

This policy supersedes any prior fair lending policy. Where this policy conflicts with applicable law or a binding regulatory order, the law or order controls; the Fair Lending Officer must promptly update this policy to eliminate the conflict.

## 3. Definitions

**Prohibited Basis.** Race, color, religion, national origin, sex (including sexual orientation and gender identity), marital status, age (provided the applicant has the capacity to contract), receipt of public assistance income, the good-faith exercise of rights under the Consumer Credit Protection Act, and, for residential real estate transactions, familial status and disability under the Fair Housing Act.

**Disparate Treatment.** Differential treatment of an applicant on a prohibited basis. Disparate treatment may be overt (an explicit policy or statement) or comparative (similarly situated applicants treated differently with no legitimate, non-discriminatory reason).

**Disparate Impact.** A facially neutral policy or practice that has a disproportionately adverse effect on a prohibited basis group, where the policy or practice is not justified by a legitimate business need that cannot be reasonably achieved by a less discriminatory alternative.

**Redlining.** The practice of providing unequal access to credit, or unequal terms of credit, to a geography on a prohibited basis, including avoiding majority-minority census tracts in the Bank's marketing, branch placement, lending, or assessment area definition.

**Steering.** Directing or encouraging an applicant toward a higher-cost, less favorable, or different product on a prohibited basis, including a refusal to discuss a product for which the applicant is qualified.

**Comparative File Review.** A statistical and manual analysis of similarly situated applicants that compares outcomes across control and prohibited-basis groups to identify unexplained disparities.

**Special Purpose Credit Program (SPCP).** A credit program established and administered consistent with 12 C.F.R. 1002.8 and CFPB guidance, designed to extend credit to an economically disadvantaged class of persons that would not be served, or would be served on less favorable terms, by the Bank's standard programs.

**Reportable HMDA Loan or Application Register (LAR) Record.** An application, origination, purchase, or denial record meeting the criteria for HMDA reporting under Regulation C.

## 4. Governance

The Bank's Fair Lending Officer reports to the Chief Compliance Officer and has functional authority to direct the design, monitoring, and remediation activities of the fair lending program across every line of business. The Fair Lending Officer has unrestricted access to applicant-level, loan-level, and HMDA data, and to the personnel and vendors involved in marketing, underwriting, pricing, and servicing.

The Fair Lending Committee, chaired by the Fair Lending Officer and including the heads of each consumer lending line of business, the Chief Risk Officer, the General Counsel or designee, the Director of Marketing, and the head of HMDA Operations, meets at least quarterly. The Committee approves the annual fair lending risk assessment, the annual monitoring plan, the design of any SPCP, and the remediation plan for any matter requiring action.

The Board Risk Committee receives a fair lending program report at least quarterly, including findings, remediation status, regulatory matters, and any change in the fair lending risk profile of the Bank.

## 5. Marketing and Outreach

Marketing, advertising, and outreach activities must reach the Bank's full assessment area and the Bank's full prospective customer base on a nondiscriminatory basis. The Marketing function, with the Fair Lending Officer's review, ensures that:

- Imagery and language used in advertising do not signal preference for, or exclusion of, any prohibited basis group;
- Targeting, look-alike modeling, and audience segmentation criteria, including criteria provided to digital advertising platforms, are reviewed for fair lending risk and do not directly or indirectly proxy for a prohibited basis;
- Spanish-language and other non-English marketing is provided where the Bank's customer base or assessment area data indicates a meaningful population of limited-English-proficient consumers;
- The Bank does not exclude any geography within its assessment area from advertising on a prohibited basis;
- Co-marketing, sponsorship, and community partnership activity is distributed to reach majority-minority and low-and-moderate-income geographies in proportion to the Bank's overall presence in the assessment area, at minimum.

Pre-screening criteria used for solicitations are reviewed by the Fair Lending Officer before launch and at least annually thereafter.

## 6. Underwriting

Underwriting decisions must be made on the basis of factors that are documented in writing, applied consistently, and predictive of credit performance. Underwriters and any automated underwriting system used by the Bank must rely only on factors that are permissible under ECOA, Regulation B, and the Fair Housing Act.

The Bank prohibits:

- Requesting or considering information about a prohibited basis except as expressly required or permitted by Regulation B (for example, government monitoring information collected for HMDA or self-test purposes);
- Imposing different documentation, verification, income calculation, or assets-required standards on similarly situated applicants on a prohibited basis;
- Refusing to consider income from part-time employment, public assistance, child support, alimony, or maintenance to the extent the income meets the Bank's general income-stability criteria;
- Discounting or refusing to aggregate income of a spouse, joint applicant, or co-borrower on a prohibited basis;
- Applying age in a way that does not satisfy the empirically derived, demonstrably and statistically sound credit scoring system requirements of Regulation B, or that disadvantages applicants 62 or older.

Exceptions to underwriting standards must be documented in the loan file with the specific reason for the exception and the approving authority. Exceptions are subject to comparative monitoring under Section 10 to confirm they are granted consistently across prohibited-basis groups.

## 7. Pricing

Pricing of consumer credit products must be based on documented, objective, and risk-related factors. Where the Bank permits employee or third-party discretion in pricing (for example, broker compensation in mortgage, dealer participation in indirect auto, or relationship-based fee waivers), the Bank establishes:

- Written pricing policies stating the permissible range of variation and the legitimate business reasons that may justify variation;
- A maximum permitted variation expressed in basis points or dollars;
- Required documentation in the loan file for any deviation from the standard pricing grid;
- Surveillance of pricing variation by prohibited-basis status of the applicant, conducted no less than quarterly by the Fair Lending Officer.

The Bank does not permit pricing to vary on a prohibited basis under any circumstance. Where surveillance identifies a statistically significant unexplained pricing disparity, the Bank reviews the underlying loan files, identifies any legitimate business factor that explains the disparity, and, if no such factor is identified, remediates affected consumers through pricing adjustment, refund of fees, or other restitution.

## 8. Steering and Product Placement

The Bank's product placement and referral practices must direct applicants to the product for which they are qualified and that meets the applicant's stated needs. Loan officers, brokers, and indirect dealer partners may not direct or encourage an applicant on a prohibited basis to a higher-cost product, a non-prime product, an indirect channel, or a product with less favorable terms than the applicant qualifies for.

Where the Bank operates a prime and a non-prime mortgage channel, or distinct credit card products with different rates and fees, the Bank monitors the channel and product mix of applicants by prohibited-basis status and investigates any unexplained disparity in placement.

## 9. Redlining and Geographic Distribution

The Bank's assessment area, branch network, marketing footprint, and lending distribution are reviewed annually by the Fair Lending Officer for redlining risk. Reviews include:

- Comparison of the Bank's application and origination distribution by census tract minority composition with peer institutions in the same MSA;
- Comparison of the Bank's branch placement and ATM distribution against demographic patterns within the assessment area;
- Review of any deserts in the Bank's lending or branch footprint that correlate with majority-minority or low-and-moderate-income geographies;
- Review of mortgage broker and indirect dealer relationships for geographic concentration that excludes majority-minority areas.

Findings of geographic disparity that cannot be explained by legitimate business factors are escalated to the Fair Lending Committee for remediation, which may include changes in branch strategy, marketing, broker relationships, or the establishment of a Special Purpose Credit Program subject to Section 12.

## 10. Comparative File Review and Statistical Monitoring

The Fair Lending Officer conducts statistical and comparative file analysis on each consumer credit product no less than annually, and more frequently for products identified as elevated risk in the annual fair lending risk assessment. Analyses include:

- Application denial rates by prohibited-basis status, controlling for credit-relevant factors;
- Pricing distribution by prohibited-basis status;
- Underwriting exception rates by prohibited-basis status;
- Origination distribution by census tract minority composition and income level;
- For HMDA-reportable products, analysis using the HMDA LAR enriched with credit and pricing variables not reported on the LAR.

Where statistical analysis identifies a prima facie disparity, the Fair Lending Officer conducts a comparative file review of similarly situated applicants. If the file review confirms an unexplained disparity, the Bank conducts a root cause analysis, develops a remediation plan, and presents the matter to the Fair Lending Committee.

## 11. Servicing, Loss Mitigation, and Collections

Fair lending obligations apply throughout the loan lifecycle. Loss mitigation, modification, forbearance, repayment plan, deed-in-lieu, short sale, and foreclosure decisions must be made on documented, consistent, and non-discriminatory criteria. The servicing and collections functions are subject to the same comparative monitoring as underwriting, including review of denial rates, modification approval rates, foreclosure timelines, and fee waivers by prohibited-basis status of the borrower.

Communications with borrowers in default must comply with the Bank's UDAAP and complaint handling policies and may not be conducted in a manner that targets or excludes any prohibited-basis group.

## 12. Special Purpose Credit Programs

The Bank may establish a Special Purpose Credit Program consistent with 12 C.F.R. 1002.8 to extend credit to an economically disadvantaged class of persons that would not be served, or would be served on less favorable terms, by the Bank's standard programs. Each SPCP must:

- Be supported by a written plan that identifies the class to be served, the credit need being addressed, and the empirical basis for the program;
- Be approved by the Fair Lending Committee and by the General Counsel for legal sufficiency;
- Have specified eligibility criteria, terms, and a defined duration with a scheduled program review;
- Be monitored for performance against its stated objectives, with adjustments made as warranted.

SPCPs are not exceptions to the Bank's fair lending obligations. Operation of an SPCP does not relieve the Bank of any obligation under ECOA, Regulation B, the Fair Housing Act, or this policy.

## 13. HMDA Data Integrity

The Bank reports HMDA LAR data accurately and on time. The HMDA Operations function operates a documented data integrity process including pre-submission scrubbing, sampling-based file review against source documents, and reconciliation of the LAR to the loan origination system. The Fair Lending Officer reviews HMDA data integrity findings before submission and any HMDA data resubmission required by the Bank's regulator.

Inaccurate HMDA reporting that is identified internally is corrected and, where required, resubmitted promptly. Persistent or material HMDA data integrity issues are reported to the Fair Lending Committee and the Board Risk Committee.

## 14. Compliance Monitoring

In addition to the targeted analyses described in Section 10, the Fair Lending Officer maintains an annual monitoring plan covering each product, channel, and stage of the credit lifecycle. The monitoring plan is documented, risk-based, and reviewed annually by the Fair Lending Committee. Internal Audit performs an independent review of the fair lending program at least every eighteen months, with findings reported to the Board Risk Committee.

## 15. Training

All employees with credit decisioning, pricing, marketing, servicing, or collections responsibilities receive fair lending training upon hire and annually thereafter. Training is role-based and addresses, at minimum, the prohibited bases under ECOA and the Fair Housing Act, the prohibitions on disparate treatment, disparate impact, redlining, and steering, the Bank's documentation expectations, and the employee's obligation to escalate concerns. Completion is tracked and reported to the Fair Lending Committee.

## 16. Violations and Enforcement

Any employee, officer, director, or contractor who engages in disparate treatment, steering, redlining, retaliation against an applicant, customer, or co-worker for raising a fair lending concern, or interference with a fair lending investigation is in violation of this policy. Violations are subject to discipline up to and including termination, and may be referred to law enforcement or to the Bank's regulator where required by law.

Material findings, including any pattern or practice issue, any matter requiring remediation to consumers, or any matter that may give rise to a referral under ECOA Section 706(g) by the Bank's regulator, must be reported by the Fair Lending Officer to the General Counsel and the Chair of the Board Risk Committee within five business days of identification.
