## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes how Meridian Trust Bank, N.A. ("the Bank") manages its overdraft program, including the payment of consumer transactions that would cause a negative balance in a consumer Asset Account, the imposition of overdraft and non-sufficient funds fees, the marketing and disclosure of overdraft services, and the supervisory monitoring of overdraft activity.

The policy is designed to comply with Regulation E Section 1005.17 (affirmative opt-in for one-time debit card and ATM overdraft services), Regulation DD Section 1030.11 (periodic statement and account-opening disclosures of overdraft and returned-item fees), the FDIC Final Overdraft Payment Supervisory Guidance (FIL-81-2010), the FFIEC Joint Guidance on Overdraft Protection Programs (70 Fed. Reg. 9127), the Bank's broader Regulation E policy (POL-REGE-001), the Bank's Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices controls under POL-UDAAP-001, and applicable fair lending considerations under POL-FAIR-001.

Overdraft incidents, opt-in coverage, fee waivers, sustained-overdraft escalations, and excessive-use determinations are reported to the Office of Consumer Compliance, the Consumer Compliance Committee, and the Board of Directors through the consumer compliance program. Overdraft-related consumer complaints feed POL-COMP-001 and Reg E error-resolution overlap is governed by POL-REGE-001.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to every consumer Asset Account at the Bank that may incur a negative balance, including consumer checking accounts, money market deposit accounts that allow third-party payments, and similar consumer transaction accounts. It applies to every channel through which a transaction may overdraw such an Account (in-branch teller, ATM withdrawal, point-of-sale debit card transaction, recurring debit card transaction, ACH debit, paper check, online or mobile bill pay, telephone-initiated transfer, and on-us / off-us automated payments) and to every employee, officer, contractor, and authorized third party that participates in account opening, opt-in capture, transaction processing, fee assessment, posting order configuration, marketing, complaint handling, or related controls.

This policy supersedes any prior overdraft policy. Where this policy conflicts with applicable law, regulation, or supervisory guidance, the law or guidance controls. Domain-specific policies (POL-REGE-001 Reg E electronic fund transfers; POL-COMP-001 consumer complaint handling; POL-UDAAP-001 unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices; POL-FAIR-001 fair lending; POL-VRM-001 vendor management; POL-IS-001 information security) operate within this framework and inherit its definitions where consistent.

## 3. Definitions

**Asset Account** has the meaning given in 12 C.F.R. 1005.2(b): an account established for any purpose primarily for personal, family, or household use that is held by a financial institution and from which electronic fund transfers may be made.

**Overdraft Service** has the meaning given in 12 C.F.R. 1005.17(a): a service under which a financial institution assesses a fee or charge on a Consumer's Asset Account for paying a transaction (including a check or other item) when the Consumer has insufficient or unavailable funds in the Account, but does not include any payment of overdrafts pursuant to a line of credit subject to Regulation Z, a service that transfers funds from another Account, or a covered separate credit feature accessible by a hybrid prepaid-credit card.

**One-Time Debit Card Transaction** means a non-recurring debit card purchase (point-of-sale or card-not-present), including ATM withdrawals, that under 12 C.F.R. 1005.17 may not be paid into overdraft and assessed an overdraft fee unless the Consumer has affirmatively opted in.

**Recurring Debit Card Transaction** means a debit card transaction authorized by the Consumer to recur at substantially regular intervals (e.g., subscription, utility, insurance payment) and is outside the scope of the 1005.17 opt-in (the Bank may pay or return such transactions under its general overdraft framework, subject to disclosed fees).

**Affirmative Consent (Opt-In)** has the meaning given in 12 C.F.R. 1005.17(b): the Consumer's express, affirmative agreement to the Bank's payment of one-time debit card and ATM transactions that would cause an overdraft, given after the Consumer has been provided with the segregated A-9 model notice required by 1005.17(d), and recorded in a manner that allows the Bank to demonstrate the Consumer's choice.

**Overdraft Fee** means a fee assessed on a Consumer's Asset Account for a transaction paid into overdraft.

**NSF Fee (Returned-Item Fee)** means a fee assessed on a Consumer's Asset Account for a transaction returned unpaid because of insufficient or unavailable funds.

**Sustained Overdraft Fee** means a fee imposed because an Account remains in a negative balance for a defined period of time after the original overdraft.

**Available Balance** means the balance after holds, pending authorizations, deposits subject to Regulation CC funds availability, and other reductions for items not yet posted; the Bank uses Available Balance (not ledger balance) at the time of authorization and again at the time of settlement to determine whether a transaction overdraws the Account.

**Authorize Positive, Settle Negative ("APSN")** means the practice in which a one-time debit card transaction is authorized against a positive Available Balance but settles against a negative balance because of intervening transactions, and the institution then assesses an overdraft fee. The OCC, FDIC, and CFPB have flagged APSN as a UDAAP risk; this policy prohibits APSN fees (Section 8).

**Re-Presentment Fee** means a fee imposed when a previously returned ACH or check item is presented again by the originator and again rejected for insufficient funds. The CFPB has flagged surprise re-presentment NSF fees as a UDAAP risk; this policy prohibits multiple NSF fees on the same item (Section 8).

**Excessive Use** means a pattern of overdraft activity by a Consumer that meets the thresholds in Section 11 and triggers outreach, fee mitigation, and consideration of more appropriate products.

## 4. Governance and Roles

The Director of Deposit Operations, reporting to the Chief Compliance Officer, owns this policy, the Bank's overdraft program design, fee schedule, opt-in mechanics, posting order, and monitoring framework, with annual Board review and approval. The Office of Consumer Compliance reviews disclosures (account-opening, A-9 opt-in, periodic statement, web/mobile, marketing), monitors Excessive Use, APSN risk, re-presentment risk, and fair lending risk, and reports quarterly to the Consumer Compliance Committee. Overdraft- and NSF-related complaints are routed through POL-COMP-001 with Reg E error-resolution overlap escalated to POL-REGE-001. The Director of Marketing reviews any overdraft marketing for compliance with the FFIEC Joint Guidance, FDIC FIL-81-2010, and POL-UDAAP-001. The General Counsel's Office reviews material program changes (fee schedule, opt-in script, posting order, sustained-overdraft trigger, de minimis threshold, daily fee cap, Excessive Use thresholds) before implementation.

## 5. Affirmative Opt-In for One-Time Debit Card and ATM Overdrafts (Reg E Section 1005.17)

The Bank does not assess an Overdraft Fee for any One-Time Debit Card Transaction or ATM withdrawal on a Consumer's Asset Account unless and until the Consumer has provided Affirmative Consent that satisfies all four of the following requirements drawn from 12 C.F.R. 1005.17(b)(1):

1. The Bank has provided the Consumer with a notice in writing or electronically that describes the Bank's Overdraft Service, in a form substantially similar to Model Form A-9 in Appendix A to Regulation E. The notice is segregated from all other information and contains the elements required by 1005.17(d): identification of the service, fees imposed, daily limits, alternative plans (if any), an explanation of the Consumer's right to opt in or not opt in, and the Bank's contact information.
2. The Bank has provided a reasonable opportunity for the Consumer to affirmatively consent.
3. The Consumer has affirmatively consented to the Bank's payment of One-Time Debit Card Transactions and ATM withdrawals into overdraft.
4. The Bank has provided the Consumer with confirmation of the Consumer's consent in writing or electronically, including a statement informing the Consumer of the right to revoke consent.

The Bank captures opt-in through a documented channel (in-branch signed consent, online click-through with confirmation, mobile flow, telephone opt-in with recording, or mailed and returned form). The Consumer's choice and timestamp are stored on the Account record. The Bank does not pre-check, default, bundle, or condition any other product, service, fee, or account term (interest rate, non-overdraft fees, minimum balance) on the Consumer's opt-in choice. The Consumer may revoke opt-in at any time, in any channel offered for opt-in, with revocation taking effect as soon as reasonably practicable and no later than the next business day, and is confirmed in writing or electronically.

For a Consumer who has not affirmatively opted in, no Overdraft Fee is assessed on a One-Time Debit Card Transaction or ATM withdrawal; the transaction is declined at authorization without fee. Recurring Debit Card Transactions, checks, ACH debits, and other items not within 1005.17 are paid or returned under the Bank's general overdraft framework and the Consumer's account agreement, subject to disclosed fees.

## 6. Account-Opening, A-9, and Periodic Statement Disclosures (Reg DD Section 1030.11)

At account opening, the Bank discloses to each Consumer the categories of transactions that may overdraw the Account, the Overdraft Fee and NSF Fee amounts, the maximum number of fees that may be assessed per day (the "daily fee cap" -- see Section 8), the de minimis threshold (Section 8), the right to opt out of the Overdraft Service for One-Time Debit Card Transactions and ATM withdrawals as required by 1005.17, and the existence of alternative options (linked deposit account transfer, linked credit line, automatic savings sweep).

The A-9 opt-in notice is delivered separately from and in addition to the account-opening disclosures. It is provided before opt-in is solicited in any channel, and it is segregated from all other information, with no pre-checked boxes, no default opt-in, and no representations that opting in is recommended, advised, beneficial, or required.

Periodic statements for each Consumer Asset Account state, in accordance with 12 C.F.R. 1030.11(a):

- The total dollar amount of all fees imposed on the Account for the statement period for paying overdrafts, separately disclosed as "Total Overdraft Fees."
- The total dollar amount of all fees imposed on the Account for the statement period for returning items unpaid, separately disclosed as "Total Returned Item Fees."
- The corresponding totals for the calendar year to date.

The Bank uses these exact captions on every Consumer periodic statement and does not aggregate these totals with any other fees.

The Bank's website and mobile app disclose, on the page from which a Consumer may open or convert to a Consumer Asset Account, the Overdraft Fee and NSF Fee amounts, the daily fee cap, the de minimis threshold, and a link to the A-9 notice and to the Consumer's opt-in status. Marketing communications that mention overdraft comply with 12 C.F.R. 1030.8 advertising rules and Section 12 of this policy.

## 7. Posting Order

The Bank's posting order is documented, applied uniformly to all Consumer Asset Accounts, and disclosed at account opening in plain language. Within each business day, transactions are posted as follows:

1. Credits (deposits, incoming wires, ACH credits, returns of prior debits) before debits.
2. Debits posted in the order in which the Bank received the authorization or item, using the Bank's processing date and time. Where multiple debits arrive in the same processing batch, items are posted by transaction type (real-time debits in the order received, then ACH debits in the order received, then checks in the order received).
3. Sustained-overdraft and overdraft fees post after all customer-initiated transactions for the day.

The Bank does not use a high-to-low posting order for Consumer Asset Accounts. The Bank does not restructure posting order for the purpose of maximizing Overdraft Fees, and the Director of Deposit Operations re-validates the production posting order against the documented order at least quarterly.

## 8. Fee Structure and Limits

**Overdraft Fee.** $35 per item paid into overdraft, subject to the limits in this Section.

**NSF Fee.** $35 per item returned unpaid for insufficient or unavailable funds, subject to the limits in this Section.

**De Minimis Threshold.** No Overdraft Fee or NSF Fee is assessed if the Account's negative Available Balance after the transaction is $5 or less, or if the transaction itself is $5 or less. The threshold is applied per item.

**Daily Fee Cap.** No more than four (4) Overdraft Fees and/or NSF Fees in any combination are assessed on a single Consumer Asset Account on any single business day.

**Sustained Overdraft Fee.** A single sustained-overdraft fee of $25 is assessed if the Account remains in a negative balance for seven (7) or more consecutive calendar days, with no further sustained-overdraft fee for that overdraft event. Notice is sent to the Consumer at day three (3) of the negative balance prompting the Consumer to bring the Account positive and identifying alternatives.

**APSN Prohibition.** No Overdraft Fee is assessed where the Bank authorized a One-Time Debit Card Transaction against a positive Available Balance and the transaction subsequently settled against a negative balance because of intervening transactions. Authorization-time Available Balance is determinative for APSN exclusion. The deposit operations system enforces this rule programmatically; manual overrides require Director of Deposit Operations approval and are reported to the Consumer Compliance Committee quarterly.

**No Multiple Fees on the Same Item.** The Bank assesses no more than one NSF Fee on the same ACH or check item across all presentments, regardless of how many times the originator re-presents the item. The deposit operations system tracks item identity (originator, amount, account, item identifier) and suppresses any second-or-later fee on the same item. Manual overrides require Director of Deposit Operations approval and are reported to the Consumer Compliance Committee quarterly.

**No Fee Without Opted-In Consent for One-Time Debit Card / ATM.** The deposit operations system enforces 12 C.F.R. 1005.17 by suppressing Overdraft Fees on One-Time Debit Card Transactions and ATM withdrawals for Consumers who have not opted in. Such transactions are declined at authorization without fee.

Fee changes (amount, daily cap, sustained-overdraft trigger, de minimis threshold) require Consumer Compliance Committee approval, General Counsel review, 30-day advance change-in-terms notice to existing Consumers under 12 C.F.R. 1030.5, and updated A-9 disclosure where opt-in mechanics are affected.

## 9. Alternatives to the Overdraft Service

The Bank offers and discloses, at account opening and at every Excessive Use outreach (Section 11), the following lower-cost alternatives:

- **Linked-Account Transfer.** Automatic transfer of available funds from a linked savings, money market, or other Asset Account to cover an item that would otherwise overdraw the Consumer's Asset Account. Transfer fee: $0.
- **Linked Line of Credit.** A consumer line of credit (subject to Regulation Z) from which advances cover items that would otherwise overdraw the Consumer's Asset Account. Subject to underwriting and disclosed APR, finance charges, and credit terms.
- **Low-Balance Alerts.** Free SMS, push, and email alerts when the Consumer's Available Balance falls below thresholds the Consumer selects.
- **Real-Time Funds Availability Display.** The mobile and online channels display the Consumer's Available Balance, including pending authorizations and Reg CC holds, on every login screen.

The Director of Deposit Operations evaluates Consumer take-up of alternatives at least annually and adjusts marketing of those alternatives to support consumer use.

## 10. Marketing and Advertising

Marketing of the Overdraft Service complies with 12 C.F.R. 1030.8 (advertising clear-and-conspicuous and accuracy standards), the FFIEC Joint Guidance, FDIC FIL-81-2010, and POL-UDAAP-001. The Bank does not:

- Promote payment of overdrafts in a manner that encourages routine or intentional use.
- Describe the Overdraft Service as "free," as a "line of credit," as "protection," or as functionally equivalent to a credit product.
- Imply that opting in to the Reg E 1005.17 service is required, recommended, or default.
- Market the Overdraft Service in or alongside marketing that targets economically vulnerable populations in a manner inconsistent with fair lending considerations under POL-FAIR-001.

All overdraft-related marketing is reviewed by the Director of Marketing and the Office of Consumer Compliance before publication, with sign-off recorded.

## 11. Excessive Use Monitoring and Mitigation

The Office of Consumer Compliance monitors each Consumer Asset Account for Excessive Use, defined as either:

- Six (6) or more Overdraft Fees plus NSF Fees in any rolling 12-month period, or
- A pattern of recurring overdraft within any rolling 90-day period (three or more separate overdraft events in 90 days).

When a Consumer crosses an Excessive Use threshold, the Bank:

1. Sends a written communication to the Consumer disclosing the year-to-date fee total, listing the alternatives in Section 9, and providing a contact for financial-counseling referral.
2. Suspends Sustained Overdraft Fees on that Account for 90 days following the communication.
3. Reviews the Account for steering toward a more appropriate product, including a checkless / no-overdraft account configuration that the Bank offers and that does not permit overdrafts and does not assess Overdraft or NSF Fees.
4. Records the outreach and any Account-configuration change.

Aggregate Excessive Use monitoring is reviewed quarterly by the Consumer Compliance Committee and tested annually for fair lending impact under POL-FAIR-001 (geographic, demographic-proxy, and product-segment analysis).

## 12. Consumer Complaint and Reg E Error-Resolution Overlap

Consumer complaints about overdraft and NSF fees are routed through POL-COMP-001. Where a complaint asserts an unauthorized electronic fund transfer, an incorrect electronic fund transfer, or other Reg E error within the meaning of 12 C.F.R. 1005.11, the matter is processed under POL-REGE-001 with the regulatory timing controls (10/45/90 days, provisional credit, written results) applied. A consumer's overdraft- or NSF-related dispute that constitutes a Reg E error claim may not be denied or delayed by routing it solely as an overdraft complaint.

Where investigation finds that a fee was assessed in violation of this policy, applicable law, or the account agreement, the Bank refunds the fee, removes any associated negative-balance effect (including any sustained-overdraft fee triggered by the disputed event), corrects the periodic statement, and reports the corrective action to the Office of Consumer Compliance.

## 13. Servicemember and Military Lending Considerations

The Bank applies the protections of the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act and the Military Lending Act to any overdraft-related credit feature where applicable, including covered borrowers. The Bank does not market the Overdraft Service in a manner that targets servicemember populations and does not condition any Account terms on opt-in for servicemember Consumers.

## 14. Recordkeeping

The Bank retains, for each Consumer Asset Account: the A-9 opt-in notice version, the channel/timestamp of any opt-in or revocation, and the confirmation provided to the Consumer, for the life of the Account plus five (5) years after closure; the fee schedule and posting-order documentation in effect on each business day, for five (5) years after the day; all periodic statements, account-opening disclosures, change-in-terms notices, Excessive Use outreach communications, sustained-overdraft notices, and complaint records, for at least five (5) years; and every override of the APSN and re-presentment controls (approver, justification, Account) for five (5) years. The 24-month minimums in Reg E and Reg DD are floors only; this policy applies the longer five-year retention.

## 15. Compliance Monitoring

The Office of Consumer Compliance performs the following continuous monitoring controls and reports results to the Consumer Compliance Committee at least quarterly:

- 1005.17 opt-in coverage: Overdraft Fees on One-Time Debit Card / ATM transactions for non-opted-in Accounts (target: zero); A-9 disclosure currency across all channels.
- Posting-order conformance: production posting order matches the documented order with no day-of-business deviation.
- Control exception monitoring: APSN-suppressed fees, re-presentment-suppressed fees, manual overrides, daily-fee-cap exceedances (target: zero), and fees assessed inside the de minimis exclusion (target: zero).
- Sustained-overdraft accuracy: trigger date, $25 amount, single fee per overdraft event.
- Excessive Use outreach completion within five business days, and periodic-statement disclosure of "Total Overdraft Fees" and "Total Returned Item Fees" for the period and year-to-date on every Consumer Asset Account.
- Marketing review sign-off, fair lending analysis under POL-FAIR-001 (geographic / demographic-proxy / product-segment), consumer complaint trend (volume, resolution, Reg E overlap), and POL-VRM-001 flow-down for any third-party deposit operations or marketing vendor.

## 16. Training

All deposit operations, branch, contact center, marketing, and consumer compliance staff complete role-tailored training within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter, covering the Reg E 1005.17 opt-in framework, the Reg DD 1030.11 disclosure requirements, the FFIEC Joint Guidance, FDIC FIL-81-2010, the APSN and re-presentment prohibitions, the de minimis threshold and daily fee cap, the sustained-overdraft and Excessive Use frameworks, the Reg E error-resolution overlap, and the marketing standards. Completion is tracked by the Office of Consumer Compliance.

## 17. Violations and Enforcement

Violations of this policy are addressed through the Bank's progressive discipline framework (counseling, written warning, performance improvement plan, suspension, termination) commensurate with severity and intent. Willful violations, falsification of opt-in records, manipulation of posting order, suppression of complaint or Reg E error records, or systemic override of APSN / re-presentment controls are grounds for immediate termination and referral to the General Counsel's Office for evaluation of further action, including referral to law enforcement. The Bank does not retaliate against any employee, officer, contractor, or third-party representative who in good faith raises an overdraft-program concern, files a complaint, refuses to assess a fee that this policy prohibits, or cooperates with internal or external review.
