## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes how Meridian Trust Bank, N.A. ("the Bank") complies with the Electronic Fund Transfer Act (EFTA), 15 U.S.C. 1693 et seq., and its implementing Regulation E, 12 C.F.R. Part 1005, which is administered by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB). Regulation E governs the rights, liabilities, and responsibilities of consumers and financial institutions in electronic fund transfers (EFTs) involving consumer accounts, including unauthorized transactions, error resolution, periodic statements, disclosures, and consumer opt-in requirements for overdraft services on one-time debit card and ATM transactions.

The policy is a primary input to the Bank's Compliance Management System (CMS). Errors, complaints, and unauthorized-transaction claims handled under this policy feed the consumer complaint program (POL-COMP-001), the operational risk program, and the UDAAP monitoring program (12 U.S.C. 5531/5536).

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to every consumer asset account held at the Bank that is established primarily for personal, family, or household purposes and from or to which EFTs may be initiated, including consumer checking, savings, money market, and prepaid accounts within the meaning of 12 C.F.R. 1005.2(b)(3). It applies to EFTs initiated by debit card, automated teller machine (ATM), point-of-sale (POS) terminal, automated clearing house (ACH), telephone bill-pay, person-to-person transfer, mobile wallet, recurring preauthorized debit, and any other electronic mechanism that debits or credits a covered account, regardless of the amount.

The policy applies to all employees, officers, directors, contractors, and third-party service providers who originate, authorize, monitor, dispute, refund, or report on EFTs on the Bank's behalf. It also applies to remittance transfers covered by Subpart B of Regulation E (12 C.F.R. 1005.30 et seq.) when those transfers are sent in the normal course of business by a remittance transfer provider; remittance-specific procedures are maintained as an addendum to this policy.

This policy supersedes any prior Regulation E policy. Where this policy conflicts with applicable law, regulation, or supervisory guidance, the law or guidance controls; the Deposit Operations Compliance Manager must promptly update this policy to eliminate the conflict.

## 3. Definitions

**Electronic Fund Transfer (EFT).** Any transfer of funds initiated through an electronic terminal, telephone, computer, or magnetic tape that orders, instructs, or authorizes the Bank to debit or credit a consumer's account, as defined in 12 C.F.R. 1005.3(b). Excluded transfers (check-derived ACH conversions covered by separate rules, certain securities and commodities transfers, intra-institutional telephone transfers not under a written plan, etc.) are listed in 12 C.F.R. 1005.3(c) and are out of scope.

**Access Device.** A card, code, or other means of access to a consumer's account, or any combination thereof, that may be used to initiate EFTs. A debit card, ATM card, mobile wallet credential, online banking password, and PIN are each Access Devices.

**Unauthorized EFT.** An EFT from a consumer's account initiated by a person other than the consumer without actual authority to initiate the transfer and from which the consumer receives no benefit. Per 12 C.F.R. 1005.2(m), an EFT is not unauthorized if it is initiated by a person who was furnished the Access Device by the consumer (unless the consumer has notified the Bank that transfers by that person are no longer authorized), is initiated with fraudulent intent by the consumer, or is initiated by the Bank or its employee.

**Notice of Loss or Theft.** A communication from a consumer to the Bank, by any reasonable means, asserting that an Access Device has been lost or stolen or that an Unauthorized EFT may occur or has occurred. The communication is effective when the consumer takes reasonable steps to provide the Bank with the pertinent information; written confirmation is not a precondition.

**Error.** Any of the categories listed in 12 C.F.R. 1005.11(a)(1), including (a) an unauthorized EFT, (b) an incorrect EFT to or from the consumer's account, (c) an omission of an EFT from a periodic statement, (d) a computational or bookkeeping error by the Bank, (e) the consumer's receipt of an incorrect amount of money from an electronic terminal, (f) an EFT not identified in accordance with 12 C.F.R. 1005.9 or 1005.10(a), and (g) a request for documentation or additional information about an EFT.

**Provisional Credit.** A credit posted to the consumer's account, with full availability, that the Bank issues during an extended error investigation under 12 C.F.R. 1005.11(c)(2)(i) when the Bank cannot complete its investigation within 10 business days of receiving notice of an Error.

**Business Day.** Any day on which the Bank's offices are open to the public for carrying on substantially all of its business functions, as required by 12 C.F.R. 1005.2(d). The Bank's Business Day calendar is published annually by Deposit Operations.

**One-Time Debit Card or ATM Transaction.** A non-recurring debit card purchase or ATM withdrawal subject to the consumer opt-in requirements of 12 C.F.R. 1005.17. Recurring debit card transactions (e.g., subscription billing) and ACH debits are out of scope of Section 1005.17.

## 4. Initial and Change-in-Terms Disclosures

The Bank provides initial Regulation E disclosures, in a form the consumer may keep, at the time the consumer contracts for an EFT service or before the first EFT involving the consumer's account, whichever is earlier (12 C.F.R. 1005.7(a)). Initial disclosures include the consumer's liability limits for unauthorized EFTs; the telephone number and address for reporting loss, theft, or unauthorized EFTs; the Bank's business days; the types of EFTs available and any limits on frequency or dollar amount; charges; the consumer's right to receive documentation; the right to stop payment of preauthorized transfers and the procedure to do so; the Bank's liability under Section 1693h of the EFTA; circumstances under which the Bank will disclose account information to third parties; the error-resolution notice required by 12 C.F.R. 1005.7(b)(10); and ATM fee notices required by 12 C.F.R. 1005.16.

Change-in-terms notices are delivered at least 21 days before the effective date of any change that will result in increased fees, increased consumer liability, fewer types of available EFTs, or stricter limitations on transfer frequency or amount (12 C.F.R. 1005.8(a)). The annual error-resolution notice is sent each year on a single statement, or alternatively a short-form abbreviated notice is sent on each periodic statement (12 C.F.R. 1005.8(b)).

## 5. Periodic Statements and Receipts

The Bank provides a periodic statement for each monthly cycle in which an EFT has occurred, and at least quarterly if no EFT has occurred (12 C.F.R. 1005.9(b)). Each statement reflects, for every EFT during the cycle, the amount, date of posting, type of transfer, type of account, identification of the third party (for transfers other than deposits at an electronic terminal), and the location or identification of the terminal involved. Statements include the address and telephone number for inquiries and the Bank's error-resolution notice.

Receipts are made available at the time the consumer initiates an EFT at an electronic terminal, with the content required by 12 C.F.R. 1005.9(a). If the Bank elects the de minimis exception for transactions of $15 or less, any receipt provided must still comply with Section 1005.9(a) in full.

## 6. Consumer Liability for Unauthorized EFTs

Consumer liability for Unauthorized EFTs is determined under 12 C.F.R. 1005.6 and is tiered based on when the consumer provides Notice of Loss or Theft. The Bank applies the following tiers, which are load-bearing for both consumer-facing dispute decisions and supervisory examinations.

**Tier 1 -- Notice within two business days of learning of loss or theft.** Consumer liability is the lesser of $50 or the amount of Unauthorized EFTs occurring before notice (12 C.F.R. 1005.6(b)(1)).

**Tier 2 -- Notice more than two business days after learning of loss or theft, but within 60 days of transmittal of the periodic statement on which the first Unauthorized EFT appears.** Consumer liability may extend up to $500, comprising up to $50 for Unauthorized EFTs occurring before the close of two business days after the consumer learned of the loss or theft, plus up to $450 for Unauthorized EFTs occurring after the close of two business days but before notice, that the Bank establishes would not have occurred had the consumer notified the Bank within the two-business-day period (12 C.F.R. 1005.6(b)(2)).

**Tier 3 -- Failure to report Unauthorized EFTs within 60 days of transmittal of the periodic statement.** Consumer liability for Unauthorized EFTs occurring after the close of the 60-day period and before notice is unlimited, to the extent the Bank establishes that those EFTs would not have occurred had the consumer notified the Bank within the 60-day period (12 C.F.R. 1005.6(b)(3)). Liability for transfers that occurred during the 60-day period continues to be capped at $50 or $500 as set forth in Tiers 1 and 2.

The Bank does not impose liability greater than the limits set forth above. Where state law, contract, or card-network rules (e.g., Visa or Mastercard zero-liability policies) provide greater consumer protection, the more protective rule controls. Consumer negligence is not a basis for increasing liability above the Section 1005.6 caps.

## 7. Error Resolution Procedures

The Bank investigates and resolves alleged Errors in accordance with 12 C.F.R. 1005.11. The procedures below are mandatory and are tracked in the Bank's dispute case management system.

**Notice of Error.** A consumer's notice of Error is timely if received by the Bank no later than 60 days after the Bank sends the periodic statement on which the alleged Error first appears, or, for accounts with statement suppression at the consumer's request, 60 days after the documentation reflecting the Error is made available. Notice may be oral or written; if oral, the Bank may require written confirmation within 10 business days (12 C.F.R. 1005.11(b)(2)) but may not condition the start of the investigation on receipt of the writing.

**Initial Investigation Window -- 10 Business Days.** The Bank completes its investigation and determines whether an Error occurred within 10 business days of receiving the notice (12 C.F.R. 1005.11(c)(1)). The Bank then reports the results to the consumer within 3 business days of completing the investigation and corrects any Error within 1 business day of determining that an Error occurred.

**Extended Investigation Window -- 45 Calendar Days with Provisional Credit.** If the Bank cannot complete its investigation within 10 business days, it may take up to 45 calendar days from receipt of notice provided that, within the 10-business-day window, the Bank (a) provisionally credits the consumer's account in the amount of the alleged Error (including interest where applicable), (b) makes the credit fully available for the consumer's use during the investigation, and (c) provides written notice of the provisional credit within 2 business days of crediting the account (12 C.F.R. 1005.11(c)(2)). The Bank may not condition provisional credit on receipt of a signed affidavit.

**Extended Window for New Accounts, Foreign-Initiated, and POS Errors -- 90 Calendar Days.** For Errors involving a new account (within 30 days of the first deposit), foreign-initiated EFTs, or POS debit card transactions other than at the Bank's own ATMs, the 10-business-day window extends to 20 business days, and the 45-calendar-day window extends to 90 calendar days (12 C.F.R. 1005.11(c)(3)).

**Resolution and Notice.** When the Bank determines an Error occurred, it corrects the Error within 1 business day and reports the correction to the consumer in writing within 3 business days. When the Bank determines no Error occurred (or that an Error different from the consumer's allegation occurred), it reports the result in writing within 3 business days, including a notice of the consumer's right to request the documents the Bank relied on in making its determination. If a provisional credit was issued, the Bank may debit the credit only after providing 5 business days' advance notice and honoring previously authorized items as if the credit were still in place (12 C.F.R. 1005.11(d)(2)).

**Documentation Requests.** The Bank delivers requested documentation supporting a no-Error determination within a reasonable time, and at no charge to the consumer.

## 8. Overdraft Opt-In for One-Time Debit Card and ATM Transactions

The Bank may not assess any fee or charge on a consumer's account for paying a One-Time Debit Card or ATM Transaction pursuant to the Bank's overdraft service unless the Bank has (a) provided the consumer with the disclosure required by 12 C.F.R. 1005.17(d) describing the service, (b) provided a reasonable opportunity for the consumer to affirmatively consent (opt in), (c) obtained the consumer's affirmative consent, and (d) provided written confirmation of the consent including a statement informing the consumer of the right to revoke at any time. This requirement is a baseline rule; it applies regardless of whether the consumer has opted in to overdraft services on checks, ACH, or recurring debits.

Affirmative consent must be obtained for each consumer; the Bank does not bundle, presume, or default the opt-in. Telephone and online opt-in flows must produce a clear, traceable record of the consumer's election. Consumers who do not opt in receive the same account terms as opted-in consumers in all other respects (12 C.F.R. 1005.17(b)(3)). Revocation is honored as soon as reasonably practicable, and in no event later than the next business day following receipt of the revocation request.

## 9. Preauthorized Transfers and Stop-Payment Rights

Preauthorized EFTs from a consumer's account require written authorization that is signed or similarly authenticated, and the Bank provides a copy of the authorization (12 C.F.R. 1005.10(b)). The consumer may stop payment of a preauthorized EFT by ordering the Bank, orally or in writing, at least 3 business days before the scheduled transfer date (12 C.F.R. 1005.10(c)); the Bank may require written confirmation within 14 days of an oral order. Where the amount may vary, the Bank or the designated payee provides notice of the transfer amount and scheduled date at least 10 days in advance (12 C.F.R. 1005.10(d)), unless the consumer has elected to receive notice only when the amount falls outside a specified range.

## 10. ATM Disclosures and Network Fees

ATMs operated by the Bank display, on the screen and in any receipt, a notice of any fee imposed by the Bank for use of the ATM by a consumer holding an account at another institution (12 C.F.R. 1005.16). The fee notice precedes the consumer's election to proceed and provides a reasonable opportunity to cancel without incurring the fee. Fee disclosures on co-branded or third-party-operated ATMs are subject to the same standard.

## 11. Recordkeeping

The Bank retains evidence of compliance with Regulation E, including initial disclosures, change-in-terms notices, error-resolution notices, opt-in records under Section 1005.17, periodic statements, electronic terminal receipts, dispute case files, and provisional credit notices, for not less than two years from the date the disclosure is required to be made or the action is required to be taken (12 C.F.R. 1005.13(b)). Records subject to ongoing examinations, enforcement matters, or litigation are retained until the matter is closed and any applicable limitations period has expired.

## 12. Compliance Monitoring

The Office of Consumer Compliance executes a quarterly Regulation E monitoring review covering: (a) timeliness of error-resolution decisions and provisional credits, by case file sample; (b) consumer-liability tier application against the Section 1005.6 caps; (c) Section 1005.17 opt-in record completeness for new debit card accounts; (d) accuracy of periodic statement content and ATM fee disclosures; and (e) third-party service provider performance under the Bank's vendor management program (POL-VEN-001). Findings are reported to the Compliance Committee and to the Audit Committee of the Board.

The Bank also runs continuous monitoring against the dispute case management system to flag cases approaching the 10-business-day decision deadline without an extension trigger, cases past the 45- or 90-calendar-day cap, provisional credit reversals lacking the 5-business-day advance notice, and overdraft fees assessed on One-Time Debit Card or ATM Transactions where no Section 1005.17 opt-in record exists. Flagged exceptions are escalated to the Deposit Operations Compliance Manager within one business day.

## 13. Training

All employees with responsibilities under this policy complete annual Regulation E training, with role-specific modules for dispute investigators, contact center agents, branch staff, ATM/Card Operations, and Deposit Operations. New hires complete training before assuming Regulation E duties. Completion is tracked in the Bank's learning management system.

## 14. Violations and Enforcement

Violations of Regulation E expose the Bank to civil money penalties under Section 918 of the EFTA (15 U.S.C. 1693o), individual and class consumer claims under Section 916 (15 U.S.C. 1693m), and supervisory action by the CFPB and the Bank's prudential regulator. Statutory damages range from $100 to $1,000 per violation in individual actions, with class action damages capped at the lesser of $500,000 or one percent of the Bank's net worth (15 U.S.C. 1693m(a)).

Internally, employees who fail to follow this policy are subject to corrective action up to and including termination. Vendors that fail to comply are managed through the contract remediation and termination provisions of POL-VEN-001.

The Director of Consumer Compliance, in coordination with the General Counsel, is the sole approver of any decision not to issue a provisional credit, not to honor a Section 1005.17 opt-in revocation as of the next business day, or to apply consumer liability above the Tier 1 cap; such decisions are documented in the case file with the legal basis and reported to the Compliance Committee.
